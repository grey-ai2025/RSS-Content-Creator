## Formula 5: The Quick News-Jack

**Expected ER:** 4–6% (but high impression potential)
**Best format:** Text (for speed)
**Frequency:** 1× per week when relevant news breaks

### Structure
```
[Bold reframe of the news — your take, not the headline]

[2–3 sentence summary of what happened]

[The "so what" for your audience — why this matters to teams/leaders]

[Your Grey AI perspective on the implication]

[Question CTA related to the news]
```

### Key Rules for News-Jacking
- **React within 24 hours.** After that, the news cycle has moved on.
- **Never just recap.** Your audience already saw the headline. Give them the implication.
- **Connect to AI literacy.** Every news-jack should tie back to: what does this mean for how teams use AI?
- **Keep it under 120 words.** News-jacks are fast reactions, not essays.

### Worked Example
```
𝐎𝐩𝐞𝐧𝐀𝐈 𝐣𝐮𝐬𝐭 𝐦𝐚𝐝𝐞 𝐞𝐯𝐞𝐫𝐲 𝐀𝐈 𝐭𝐫𝐚𝐢𝐧𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐠𝐫𝐚𝐦 𝐨𝐛𝐬𝐨𝐥𝐞𝐭𝐞.

Or did they?

[Feature/announcement] just dropped. It's impressive. But it doesn't change the fundamental problem:

Most teams can't use the tools they already have.

Newer tools won't fix a literacy gap. They'll widen it.

The companies investing in AI training right now aren't doing it because the tools are hard. They're doing it because their people don't know what's possible.
```

## Formula 6: The Founder Story

**Expected ER:** 7–10%
**Best format:** Text
**Frequency:** 2× per month

### Structure
```
[Vulnerable/honest opening — something real that happened]

[The situation (2–3 sentences)]

[The turning point or insight]

[The lesson — stated simply]

[Connection to what Grey AI does (subtle, not salesy)]

[Reflective question CTA]
```

### Key Rules
- Keep it real. Founder stories that feel manufactured bomb.
- Show, don't tell. Describe what happened, don't just state the lesson.
- One story, one lesson. Don't try to fit three insights into one post.
- Keep under 130 words. Long founder stories lose people.

## Hook Bank (Ready to Use)

### Binary Hooks
- "Two types of teams right now. Which one are you building?"
- "Same tool. Same budget. Completely different results."
- "Most teams automate tasks. The best teams automate decisions."
- "Your team has an AI expert. That's your biggest vulnerability."
- "Everyone's adopting AI. Almost nobody's delegating to it."

### Provocative Hooks
- "Your AI strategy is a coping mechanism."
- "The best prompt engineer on your team is about to become a bottleneck."
- "AI literacy isn't a nice-to-have. It's the new baseline for employment."
- "You're not behind on AI adoption. You're behind on AI trust."
- "The companies cutting AI training budgets are the ones who'll need it most."

### Stat Gap Hooks
- "[X]% of teams say they use AI. [Y]% can name a workflow it actually changed."
- "AI reduces the performance gap by 75%. But only if people know how to use it."
- "$650B in AI infrastructure. Zero dollars in AI fluency."
- "Your team touches AI 10× a day. They delegate to it 0× a week."

### Contrarian Hooks
- "Stop training your team on prompt engineering."
- "The AI skills gap isn't about skills. It's about confidence."
- "You don't need an AI strategy. You need an AI habit."
- "Most AI training is built for engineers. 90% of your team aren't engineers."

## Post Audit Rubric

Score each post 1–5 on these dimensions before publishing:

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Average) | 5 (Strong) |

| Hook tension | Informational opener | Mild curiosity | "Stop scrolling" tension |

| Framework | No structure | Loose structure | Clear binary/contrast |
| Specificity | Generic claims | Some detail | Specific stats/names/data |

| Brand voice | Generic/corporate | Professional | Distinctly Grey AI |
