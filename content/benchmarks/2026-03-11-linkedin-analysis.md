## Pattern Analysis

### Hook Patterns
- **Provocative declaration (4/15):** Bold claims that force a reaction. Example: "The next unicorn will have one employee + AI"
- **News-jack / speed signal (3/15):** Leading with breaking news. Example: "That was quick! OpenAI has reportedly started rolling out..."
- **Contrarian reframe (3/15):** Challenging conventional thinking. Example: "The hardest AI shift isn't being more efficient..."
- **Named framework (2/15):** Introducing labeled concepts. Example: "Four agentic design patterns"
- **Numbered utility promise (2/15):** Opening with specific count of actionable items. Example: "5 prompts to supercharge your everyday workflow"
- **First-person experiment (1/15):** Personal testing narrative. Example: Mollick's Devin hands-on review
- **Observation:** Zero top posts opened with a question. The highest-engagement hooks stated strong positions or led with surprising data points.

### Format Distribution
- Text-only: 27% (4/15)
- Text with image: 27% (4/15)
- Text with linked article: 27% (4/15)
- Video (under 3 min): 13% (2/15)
- Text with carousel: 6% (1/15)

### Topic Themes
1. **AI agents and agentic architecture** — Design patterns, one-person unicorns, Devin experiments (4 posts)
2. **AI capability step-changes** — Cost collapses, "Something Big Is Happening", year-ahead predictions (4 posts)
3. **Practical AI tools and workflows** — Voice AI, prompts, which AIs to use (3 posts)
4. **AI business models and economics** — OpenAI premium pricing, revenue projections (2 posts)
5. **AI and society** — Education transformation, science crisis (2 posts)

### Structural Patterns
- **Numbered lists dominate:** 10 of 15 top posts use numbered points. Lists of 3-5 items perform best.
- **Framework posts outperform opinion posts:** Posts that name a framework or mental model generate higher comment engagement because they give readers vocabulary to adopt and repeat.
- **Short post + long-form link is a proven combo:** 4 of 15 posts use a brief LinkedIn caption linking to comprehensive content. The LinkedIn post sells the click.
- **Comment-gating drives reach:** Beliunas consistently uses "comment to get..." CTAs that force algorithmic engagement.

### Length & Engagement Correlation
- **Short posts (1-3 sentences + media) can dominate** when paired with a strong visual or stat hook
- **Medium-length posts (150-300 words) are the sweet spot** for thought leadership — long enough for substance, short enough to read without expanding
- **Long text-only posts require strong hooks and numbered structure** to hold attention
- **The linked article model bypasses length constraints** entirely — teaser on LinkedIn, depth elsewhere

## 2026 Algorithm Notes
- **External link penalty:** ~60% reach reduction for posts with outbound links (use link-in-comments or native content)
- **Carousel engagement premium:** 6.60% engagement rate vs 2-4% for other formats
- **Comments weighted 15x over likes** in algorithmic distribution
- **First 60 minutes are critical** — early engagement determines reach trajectory
- **Document/PDF posts getting 3x higher engagement** in February 2026 algorithm update

## Actionable Takeaways for Grey AI

1. **Lead with a number or a coined term, not a question.** The highest-performing hooks used stats, cost collapses, or neologisms. Zero top posts opened with a question.

2. **Name your framework.** Posts that introduced named concepts (agentic design patterns, outputs-over-inputs, vibefounding) generated disproportionate comments because readers adopt the vocabulary. Give your thesis a label.

3. **Use provocative declarations over hedged observations.** "The next unicorn will have one employee" outperforms "We might see smaller companies succeed with AI." State positions as facts.

4. **Structure with 3-5 numbered points.** Numbered observations, steps, or features are the dominant structure across all influencers. Each point should be independently valuable (scannable, not sequential).

5. **Prioritize comment-driving hooks over like-driving hooks.** With comments weighted 15x over likes in the algorithm, contrarian takes and "you're doing it wrong" framings generate more reach than inspirational content.
