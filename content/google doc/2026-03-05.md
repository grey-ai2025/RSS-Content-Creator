Its first primary target is Alex Bores, a New York Assembly member and former Palantir employee who sponsored the RAISE Act — an AI transparency law signed in December 2025 that requires large AI labs to maintain and publish safety plans and report catastrophic safety incidents. The PAC is backed by Palantir co-founder Joe Lonsdale, OpenAI President Greg Brockman, Andreessen Horowitz, and Perplexity.

Despite this, the PAC has committed to spending at least $10 million against Bores' race — extraordinary spending for a state assembly district where typical total campaign spending is under $100,000.

Meta is also deploying $65 million into two separate super PACs targeting state-level candidates friendly to the tech industry. AI companies and executives collectively donated $83 million to federal campaigns and committees in 2025. The counter-force backing Bores is an Anthropic-funded group, Public First Action, spending $450,000 — a 22:1 spending disadvantage. This is now the architecture of AI governance: not legislation, but capture of the legislators.
