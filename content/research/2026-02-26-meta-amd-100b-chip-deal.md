# Meta Strikes Up to $100B AMD Chip Deal as It Chases 'Personal Superintelligence'

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/meta-strikes-up-to-100b-amd-chip-deal-as-it-chases-personal-superintelligence/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Keyword Match:** edge AI and hardware efficiency, agentic AI, AI in the enterprise

---

## Summary

Meta announced a landmark multiyear deal to purchase up to $100 billion worth of AMD chips, representing roughly six gigawatts of data center power demand — and structured the arrangement so that AMD issued Meta a performance-based warrant for up to 160 million AMD shares (approximately 10% of the company) at $0.01 each. The deal covers AMD's MI540 GPU series and latest-generation CPUs, as Meta aggressively diversifies its compute stack away from Nvidia. CEO Mark Zuckerberg tied the purchase explicitly to Meta's ambition of building "personal superintelligence" — AI systems designed to deeply understand and empower individual users. The deal positions AMD as a credible Nvidia alternative at hyperscaler scale.

---

## Key Data Points

- Deal value: Up to $100 billion in AMD chips over multiple years
- Power demand covered: approximately 6 gigawatts of data center capacity
- Equity structure: AMD issued Meta a warrant for up to 160 million AMD shares (~10% of company) at $0.01 each, vesting on performance milestones; AMD stock would need to reach $600 for Meta to receive the final tranche (closed Monday at $196.60)
- Chip types: AMD MI540 GPU series and latest-generation CPUs
- AMD CEO Lisa Su: "The CPU market is absolutely on fire... agentic AI scales, and our portfolio is in an extremely good position"
- Mark Zuckerberg framed the deal as "an important step" toward "personal superintelligence"
- Meta has pledged to invest at least $600 billion in U.S. data centers and AI infrastructure over the next several years
- Meta's 2026 projected capex: $135 billion
- The deal follows a similar AMD-OpenAI equity-for-chips arrangement struck in October 2025
- Meta also recently signed a deal with Nvidia for its latest CPUs and GPUs

---

## Why It Matters

This is the most significant signal yet that AI chip supply is being restructured at the infrastructure level. Meta's willingness to offer AMD equity as a procurement incentive — and AMD's willingness to take it — suggests the chip market is moving toward strategic partnerships rather than spot purchases. For professional audiences tracking enterprise AI infrastructure, the story makes three things clear: hyperscalers are building in Nvidia alternatives before they need them; CPUs are becoming a first-class inference compute layer alongside GPUs; and "personal superintelligence" is now a stated corporate objective at the world's largest social media company, not just a research aspiration.
