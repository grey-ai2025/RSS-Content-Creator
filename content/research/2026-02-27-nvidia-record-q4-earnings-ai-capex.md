# Nvidia Posts Another Record Quarter as AI Capex Hits Historic Levels

**Title:** Nvidia has another record quarter amid record capex spends

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/25/nvidia-earnings-record-capex-spend-ai/

**Publication Date:** February 25, 2026

**Keyword Match:** Exception — landmark financial result; also aligns with AI in the enterprise, edge AI and hardware efficiency

## Summary

Nvidia delivered another record-breaking quarter, with profit nearly doubling to approximately $43 billion as hyperscaler customers continued pouring capital into AI infrastructure at historic rates. CEO Jensen Huang described demand for AI compute as having "gone completely exponential," framing Nvidia's position as a direct beneficiary of every major AI deployment decision made by enterprises and cloud providers. The results came alongside broader market anxieties about whether the unprecedented levels of AI capital expenditure — estimated at $2.5 trillion globally in 2026 — will translate into proportional business returns. Fast Company noted that concerns are mounting about the AI economy even as Nvidia's numbers exceed projections. The quarter cements Nvidia's status as the essential infrastructure layer for the entire AI industry, but also sharpens scrutiny on the ROI gap between AI spending and measurable productivity gains.

## Key Data Points

- Nvidia Q4 profit: approximately $43 billion — nearly double the prior year's figure
- Earnings per share: $1.76
- Jensen Huang quote: "The demand for tokens in the world has gone completely exponential"
- Global AI spending in 2026 estimated at $2.5 trillion — a 44% year-over-year increase
- That spending figure exceeds the combined inflation-adjusted cost of the Apollo and Manhattan projects
- Counter-signal: only 8% of Americans say they would pay extra for AI capabilities (ZDNet)
- Counter-signal: 80% of firms across 6,000 executives surveyed report AI has had zero measurable impact on jobs or productivity (NBER)
- Hyperscaler capex commitments to AI infrastructure remain at record highs despite ROI questions

## Why It Matters

Nvidia's earnings are a proxy for the entire AI industry's momentum. When Nvidia beats projections at this scale, it confirms that enterprise and hyperscaler AI investment has not slowed — regardless of public skepticism about ROI. For professional audiences, the story is not just about Nvidia's stock; it is about the widening gap between infrastructure spend and demonstrated business value. The $2.5 trillion being deployed in 2026 represents the largest technology infrastructure bet in history. Whether that bet pays off — and who captures the value — is the central strategic question for every enterprise leader right now.
