# AI Coding: Claude's COBOL Modernization Claim Sends IBM Stock to Its Worst Day in 25 Years

**Source URL:** https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/cobol-modernization-ai-claude-ibm/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI coding tools, agentic AI, AI in the enterprise

---

## Summary

Anthropic's claim that Claude Code can automate the exploration and analysis phases of COBOL modernization — collapsing multi-year migration timelines into quarters — triggered IBM's single worst trading day in over 25 years. COBOL is the backbone of global financial infrastructure: it handles roughly 95% of U.S. ATM transactions and hundreds of billions of lines still run daily across banking and government systems. IBM has long monetized the complexity of migrating off these systems through consulting and mainframe services. While IBM argues that "translating code is one thing; modernising a platform is something else entirely," existing evidence suggests AI is already compressing the most time-intensive phases — one client reported a 94% reduction in analysis time.

---

## Key Data Points

- IBM stock fell more than 13% in a single day — its worst single-day drop in over 25 years
- COBOL handles approximately 95% of U.S. ATM transactions
- Hundreds of billions of lines of COBOL run daily in financial and government systems
- Anthropic claims Claude Code can "automate the exploration and analysis phases that consume most of the effort in COBOL modernisation"
- Royal Bank of Canada used IBM's watsonx Code Assistant to map COBOL dependencies (AI-assisted migration already underway)
- National Organisation for Social Insurance: 94% reduction in analysis time using AI — cutting an eight-hour task to approximately 30 minutes
- IBM's counter-argument: code translation and full platform modernization are distinct challenges
- The AI-COBOL angle also put Indian IT services and BPO stocks under pressure, given those sectors' reliance on legacy system maintenance contracts
- Fast Company headline: "IBM stock falls after Anthropic says AI can modernize old software"

## Why It Matters

This story is the clearest case yet of AI coding tools creating direct, measurable market disruption to an established services industry — not a hypothetical threat but a stock-price event. For professional audiences in enterprise IT, consulting, and financial services, it raises a concrete question: which parts of your business model depend on the complexity of maintaining legacy systems? AI coding tools are not just accelerating development — they are compressing the economics of technical debt resolution. The COBOL case is a preview of a pattern that will repeat across other legacy tech verticals as AI coding capability advances.
