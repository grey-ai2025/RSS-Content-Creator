# Nvidia Challenger AI Chip Startup MatX Raised $500M

**Publication Date:** February 24–25, 2026

**Keyword Match:** edge AI and hardware efficiency, open source AI models

**Newsworthy Exception:** Major funding round ($500M) from a landmark startup directly challenging Nvidia's AI chip dominance — qualifies as an exceptionally newsworthy infrastructure story published within 48 hours.

## Summary

MatX, founded in 2023 by two former Google TPU engineers, has raised a $500 million Series B to build AI chips targeting 10x better LLM training and inference performance than Nvidia's GPUs. The round was led by Jane Street and Situational Awareness — an investment fund formed by former OpenAI researcher Leopold Aschenbrenner. Investors also include Marvell Technology and Stripe co-founders Patrick and John Collison. MatX will manufacture its chips with TSMC and plans to begin shipping in 2027. The round validates a wave of Nvidia-alternative chip startups as the AI infrastructure buildout accelerates.

- Manufacturing partner: TSMC
- Expected shipping: 2027
- Closest competitor: Etched, which raised $500M at a $5B valuation in January 2026
- CEO announcement made via LinkedIn post

## Why It Matters

The race to dethrone Nvidia is no longer a startup fantasy — it is a capital-backed infrastructure bet. A $500M round anchored by Jane Street (one of the world's most sophisticated algorithmic trading firms) and a fund founded by a former OpenAI researcher signals that the financial and technical communities see Nvidia's dominance as a structural inefficiency to be solved. For professional audiences in enterprise tech, this story matters because chip diversity determines who controls AI compute costs over the next decade. If MatX or competitors like Etched succeed, it decouples AI deployment economics from a single supplier — fundamentally changing the AI-first infrastructure calculus.
