# Anthropic Exposes Industrial-Scale Theft of Claude's Capabilities by Chinese AI Labs

**Title:** Anthropic: Claude faces 'industrial-scale' AI model distillation

**Source URL:** https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/anthropic-claude-faces-industrial-scale-ai-model-distillation/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI governance and security, open source AI models

## Summary

Anthropic has publicly documented a coordinated campaign in which Chinese AI laboratories — including DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax — conducted industrial-scale extraction of Claude's capabilities through model distillation. The operation involved over 24,000 fraudulent accounts submitting more than 16 million queries designed to systematically capture and replicate Claude's outputs for use in training competing models. This is the first time a frontier AI lab has documented and disclosed a distillation campaign of this scale. Separately, Reuters reported that DeepSeek trained its most recent model using Nvidia Blackwell GPUs that are subject to U.S. export controls — a direct violation of chip restriction policy. Together, these revelations sharpen the geopolitical dimension of the AI race: proprietary model capabilities are now targets for systematic extraction, not just competitive replication.

## Key Data Points

- Over 24,000 fake accounts were created specifically to query Claude at scale
- More than 16 million exchanges were captured in the distillation campaign
- Labs implicated: DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax — all Chinese AI developers
- Model distillation: a technique where outputs from a powerful "teacher" model are used to train a cheaper, faster "student" model — without the original lab's consent or compensation
- Reuters separately reported DeepSeek used banned Nvidia Blackwell GPUs to train its latest model, violating U.S. export controls
- Anthropic's disclosure is notable — most labs do not publicly document security breaches or IP extraction attempts of this kind
- The scale (16M queries, 24K accounts) indicates organized, resource-intensive coordination rather than opportunistic scraping

## Why It Matters

Model distillation at industrial scale is the AI equivalent of corporate espionage — and Anthropic's disclosure makes the threat visible in a way the industry has largely avoided discussing publicly. For enterprise AI buyers, this raises legitimate questions about IP chain-of-custody: if a cheaper model was trained on extracted outputs from a premium model, what are the legal and ethical implications of using it? For policy audiences, the combination of distillation campaigns and export control violations by the same players represents a significant escalation in U.S.-China AI competition. Governance frameworks that do not account for systematic capability extraction are already behind the curve.
