## Summary
Amazon expanded access to its Health AI assistant on March 10, bringing it from the One Medical app to its main website and mobile app and opening it to all users — not just Prime subscribers or One Medical members. Health AI can answer health questions, explain lab results and medical records, manage prescription renewals, book appointments with healthcare providers, and connect users to One Medical professionals for follow-up care. The assistant operates within a HIPAA-compliant environment and uses the Health Information Exchange to access a user's personal health records with permission. Amazon said it trains the underlying models on "abstracted patterns without directly identifying information." Prime members get up to five free direct-message consultations with a One Medical provider for over 30 common conditions. The launch positions Amazon in direct competition with OpenAI's ChatGPT Health (launched January 2026) and Anthropic's Claude for Healthcare (also January 2026).

## Key Data Points
- Health AI now available to all users on Amazon.com and the Amazon app — no Prime or One Medical membership required
- Originally launched only within the One Medical app following Amazon's $3.9 billion acquisition of One Medical in 2023
- Can answer general health questions, explain health records, manage prescription renewals, and book appointments
- Operates within a HIPAA-compliant environment with encryption and strict access controls
- Accesses personal health data through the Health Information Exchange with user permission
- Trains on "abstracted patterns without directly identifying information" — Amazon's stated approach to avoiding direct PII use
- Prime members in the US get up to 5 free direct-message care consultations with One Medical providers for 30+ common conditions
- Non-Prime members can access One Medical providers via a pay-per-visit option
- Launches less than 3 months after OpenAI's ChatGPT Health and Anthropic's Claude for Healthcare
- 230 million users ask ChatGPT about health each week (OpenAI statistic from January 2026 launch context)
- Amazon's entry signals major platform consolidation: health AI is now embedded in the largest consumer shopping platform in the US

## Why It Matters
Amazon distributing a health AI agent through its main app is a significant distribution moment — this is not a standalone health app requiring adoption, it is a feature embedded inside an app that hundreds of millions of people already open daily. The agentic framing matters here: Health AI does not just answer questions, it takes actions (booking appointments, managing prescriptions, connecting to providers). This is a direct demonstration of the "agents working on behalf of" pattern that defines the current AI cycle. For enterprise and healthcare professionals, the competitive dynamics are equally significant: three of the largest AI ecosystems (OpenAI, Anthropic, Amazon) all entered the consumer health AI market within the same 60-day window, signaling that health is the next high-stakes agentic deployment domain.
