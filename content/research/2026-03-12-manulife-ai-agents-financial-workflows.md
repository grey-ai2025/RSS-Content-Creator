# Manulife Moves AI Agents Into Core Financial Operations — $1 Billion in Expected Value by 2027

**Title:** Manulife moves AI agents into core financial workflows

**Source URL:** https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/manulife-moves-ai-agents-into-core-financial-workflows/

**Publication Date:** March 11, 2026

**Keyword Match:** agentic AI, AI agents, AI governance

**Summary:**
Canadian insurance giant Manulife is deploying agent-based AI systems across its core business operations — moving well beyond pilot projects into production-scale agentic AI. The company expects these AI initiatives to generate more than US$1 billion in value by 2027, primarily through productivity improvements and workflow automation in high-volume, data-intensive areas like claims processing, document review, internal reporting, and policy management. Manulife is building a runtime platform capable of supporting agentic AI systems that can execute tasks across multiple software tools and datasets simultaneously. Approximately 75% of its global workforce already uses generative AI tools in daily work, and the company plans to expand from 35+ active use cases to approximately 70.

**Key Data Points:**
- Expected value from AI initiatives: more than US$1 billion by 2027
- Current generative AI use cases in production: 35+; target: approximately 70
- Approximately 75% of Manulife's global workforce currently uses generative AI tools
- AI agents are being deployed for: document review, internal reporting, claims processing, and policy management
- The runtime platform supports agents that can collect data from internal systems and prepare summaries — reducing human time spent on information gathering
- Regulatory constraint: financial institutions require auditable systems, explainable decisions, and governance controls — Manulife's deployment strategy is built around these requirements
- Industry benchmark: McKinsey's 2024 Global AI Survey found approximately 65% of organizations use generative AI in at least one function, but most remain in pilot phase rather than full production

**Why It Matters:**
Manulife represents one of the clearest data points yet in the "from pilot to production" shift that defines enterprise AI in 2026. A $1 billion value target from agentic AI — in a highly regulated industry with strict governance requirements — demonstrates that autonomous AI agents are no longer a theoretical enterprise promise. For financial services professionals, technology leaders, and anyone building enterprise AI governance frameworks, this case study shows what mature agentic deployment looks like: a runtime platform, explainable decisions, auditable workflows, and 75% workforce adoption as the foundation — not the outcome.
