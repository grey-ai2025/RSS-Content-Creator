# Block Cuts 40% of Workforce as Jack Dorsey Goes All-In on AI Tools

**Source URL:** https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/02/block-lays-off-40-of-workforce-as-it-goes-all-in-on-ai-tools/

**Publication Date:** February 27, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI job displacement vs. job creation (#6), AI-first business strategy (#12), #FutureOfWork, #AI, #ArtificialIntelligence

---

## Summary

Jack Dorsey's Block — parent company of Square and Cash App — is cutting its workforce by nearly half, eliminating more than 4,000 of its 10,000+ employees. In a shareholder letter, Dorsey explicitly cited AI tools as the reason: "A significantly smaller team, using the tools we're building, can do more and do it better. And intelligence tool capabilities are compounding faster every week." Dorsey added that "most companies are late" to recognize this shift and predicted "a majority of companies" would follow within the next year. Block's stock surged over 25% in after-hours trading on the announcement. This makes Dorsey among the first Silicon Valley CEOs to explicitly and publicly tie mass layoffs to AI capability rather than economic downturn. Block's move comes as Amazon, UPS, Dow, Nike, and Home Depot together announced 52,000 combined job cuts in January.

- January 2026: Amazon, UPS, Dow, Nike, Home Depot announced combined 52,000 job cuts
- Context: One of the first explicit public cases of a major CEO tying AI capability gains directly to headcount reduction — not performance

---

## Why It Matters

This is the story the AI-and-jobs debate has been waiting for: a named CEO, at a major company, explicitly stating that AI capability — not financial performance, not market conditions — is why thousands of people are losing their jobs. Dorsey's prediction that "most companies" will follow within a year is the kind of concrete, directional statement that professional audiences engage with. For LinkedIn's professional community, this raises immediate questions about skills, roles, and what "AI-proof" even means in practice. The Block announcement is also significant because the company reported strong financials — this was a strategic restructuring, not a cost-cutting crisis response.
