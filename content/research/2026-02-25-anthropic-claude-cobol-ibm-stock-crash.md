# Anthropic Says Claude Code Can Modernize COBOL — IBM Stock Craters 13%

**Title:** IBM Shares Plunge as Anthropic Touts COBOL Modernization with Claude Code

**Source URL:** https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/23/ibm_share_dive_anthropic_cobol/

**Publication Date:** February 23, 2026

**Summary:**
IBM's share price fell approximately 13% — its worst single-day drop in over 25 years — after Anthropic published a blog post claiming its Claude Code tool can dramatically accelerate the modernization of legacy COBOL applications. Anthropic argued that AI can map dependencies, document workflows, and identify migration risks across COBOL codebases in ways that would take human analysts months to complete, making a process previously described as "cost-prohibitive" far more accessible. The irony is sharp: IBM itself launched a nearly identical product in 2023 called "watsonx Code Assistant for Z," designed to convert COBOL to Java using generative AI. IBM pushed back, arguing that "decades of hardware-software integration cannot be replicated by moving code." The market reaction, however, was swift and severe, erasing billions in market cap in a single session.

**Key Data Points:**
- IBM shares closed down ~13.2% to $223.35 — worst single-day decline since 2000
- Anthropic's Claude Code blog post specifically targeted COBOL modernization as a key use case
- COBOL underpins critical systems in banking, government, airlines, and retail payment processing
- Skilled COBOL programmers are increasingly scarce as the language dates to the late 1950s
- IBM introduced "watsonx Code Assistant for Z" in 2023 with the same COBOL-to-Java conversion pitch
- IBM's CEO credited AI-powered conversion tools with helping achieve the company's highest mainframe revenue in 20 years
- AWS, Microsoft, Kyndryl, and NTT have all previously launched mainframe migration products
- Infosys chairman Nandan Nilekani noted AI has made legacy application rewrites more economically feasible
- The sell-off was part of a broader market concern about AI disrupting SaaS and enterprise software incumbents, with Salesforce, Adobe, and ServiceNow also affected

**Why It Matters:**
This story is a striking case study in how AI announcements now move markets in real time. A single Anthropic blog post wiped out more than a decade of IBM mainframe goodwill with investors in a single trading session. For enterprise technology leaders, it raises a critical question: which entrenched legacy IT vendors are most exposed to AI-driven disruption? COBOL modernization is a multi-billion-dollar industry, and if AI can compress years of migration work into weeks, the economics of outsourced mainframe services — IBM's bread and butter — change fundamentally. For professionals working in enterprise IT, digital transformation, or financial services, this is a must-watch development.
