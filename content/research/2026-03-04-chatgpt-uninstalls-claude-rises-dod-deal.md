# ChatGPT Uninstalls Surge 295% as Claude Hits No. 1 on the App Store After DoD Deal

**Title:** ChatGPT uninstalls surged by 295% after DoD deal

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/02/chatgpt-uninstalls-surged-by-295-after-dod-deal/

**Publication Date:** March 3, 2026

**Keyword Match:** Anthropic vs. Pentagon escalation / Military AI ethics (Keyword 1); OpenAI Department of War agreement details (Keyword 10); AI safety race / guardrails loosening (Keyword 2)

**Summary:**
Consumer behavior data from multiple market intelligence providers confirms that OpenAI's deal with the US Department of Defense triggered a measurable, rapid market shift: US uninstalls of the ChatGPT mobile app jumped 295% day-over-day on February 28, compared to a typical 9% uninstall rate. Simultaneously, Anthropic's Claude saw US downloads rise 37% on Friday and 51% on Saturday after its public refusal of the DoD contract terms on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Claude hit No. 1 on the US App Store on February 28 — a jump of more than 20 ranks from the week prior — and the app's daily US downloads surpassed ChatGPT's for the first time, according to Appfigures. The data reveals that a segment of consumers is actively rewarding AI companies that hold safety lines, translating Anthropic's policy stance into direct commercial gains.

**Key Data Points:**
- US ChatGPT mobile app uninstalls jumped 295% day-over-day on February 28, 2026 (Sensor Tower data)
- ChatGPT's typical day-over-day uninstall rate is 9%, measured over the prior 30 days — making the 295% spike roughly 33x the norm
- ChatGPT US downloads fell 13% day-over-day on Saturday after the DoD deal was announced; fell another 5% on Sunday
- Claude US downloads rose 37% day-over-day on Friday (Feb 27) and 51% on Saturday (Feb 28)
- Claude hit No. 1 on the US App Store on February 28 — up more than 20 ranks compared to February 22
- Appfigures: Claude's total daily US downloads surpassed ChatGPT's for the first time on February 28
- Appfigures estimated Claude's download increase on Saturday at 88% day-over-day
- Claude is now the No. 1 free iPhone app in 6 countries outside the US: Belgium, Canada, Germany, Luxembourg, Norway, Switzerland
- Similarweb: Claude's US downloads for the past week were approximately 20x January levels
- 1-star reviews for ChatGPT surged 775% on Saturday; 5-star reviews dropped 50% in the same period

**Why It Matters:**
For enterprise AI buyers and strategists, this data is not just about app store rankings — it is evidence that AI safety positioning is becoming a commercial differentiator, not just a PR posture. Anthropic's refusal to loosen guardrails for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons use generated a measurable consumer market response within 48 hours. The speed and scale of this shift — from policy dispute to app store reversal in a single weekend — demonstrates that enterprise AI vendors are now operating in a market where safety brand equity can be built and destroyed rapidly. For any organization currently evaluating AI vendor relationships, the DoD episode is a live case study in how safety red lines translate into market trust (or its absence). The data also reinforces that the Anthropic/OpenAI competitive dynamic is now being shaped as much by ethics positioning as by model capability.
