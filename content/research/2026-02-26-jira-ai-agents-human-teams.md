# Jira's Latest Update Allows AI Agents and Humans to Work Side by Side

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/25/jiras-latest-update-allows-ai-agents-and-humans-to-work-side-by-side/

**Publication Date:** February 25, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI agents, agentic AI, multi-agent systems, AI in the enterprise, human-agent teams

---

## Summary

Atlassian has launched "agents in Jira," a new capability that lets teams assign tasks and tickets to AI agents from the same dashboard they use for human employees. The update gives enterprises unified visibility into AI and human workstreams — tracking progress, setting deadlines, and looping in agents mid-project. Atlassian's chief product and AI officer framed the release as solving a coordination problem: as agents do more work, the challenge shifts from deploying AI to managing it alongside people. The feature is now available in open beta.

---

## Key Data Points

- Atlassian bills the update as enabling teams to produce "10x the work without 10x the chaos"
- Agents in Jira allows enterprises to assign, track, and deadline AI agents exactly as they would human employees
- Users can loop AI agents into existing projects mid-stream, not just at kickoff
- Feature is in open beta as of February 25, 2026
- Atlassian has been in collaboration software for decades; this is its first move to treat agents as first-class team members in its core project management product
- Tamar Yehoshua, Atlassian's new chief product and AI officer, stated the key problem: "You want to be able to coordinate between humans and agents"
- Atlassian explicitly acknowledged agents are creating "more chaos" for teams and positioned this as an ordering mechanism

---

## Why It Matters

This is the most concrete enterprise signal yet that AI agents are being treated as workers — not just tools. When the world's dominant project management platform gives AI agents their own task queues and deadlines, it signals that the "human-agent team" model is moving from concept to infrastructure. For professional audiences, this reframes the AI adoption conversation: the question is no longer whether to use AI agents, but how to manage them alongside people. The ROI question — which tasks should agents own vs. humans — is now answerable in the same interface.
