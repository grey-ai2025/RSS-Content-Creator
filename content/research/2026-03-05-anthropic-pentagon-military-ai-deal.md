# Anthropic CEO Calls OpenAI's Military Deal Messaging "Straight Up Lies" as Claude Gets Used in Active Iran Strikes

## Summary

The fallout from Anthropic's refusal to allow unrestricted military use of its AI has escalated into an open war of words between the two leading AI labs. Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei sent an internal memo calling OpenAI's Pentagon deal "safety theater" and Sam Altman's public positioning "straight up lies" — accusing OpenAI of caring more about placating employees than preventing abuse. The dispute centers on nearly identical language: Anthropic demanded the DoD affirm it would not use Claude for autonomous weaponry or mass domestic surveillance; OpenAI agreed to a deal permitting use for "all lawful purposes," claiming the same protections exist in contract language.

The irony is acute. Despite being designated a "supply chain risk" and facing a directive from President Trump to wind down defense operations, Anthropic's models are still actively being used in the US-Israel aerial campaign against Iran — feeding targeting decisions through a system integrated with Palantir's Maven platform. The ban has not yet been formally executed, creating a legal gray zone in which Claude is helping plan strikes even as defense contractors like Lockheed Martin rush to swap out Anthropic models for competitors.

The public appears to be siding with Anthropic rather than the White House or OpenAI. ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% following OpenAI's Pentagon announcement, and Claude jumped from outside the top 100 to #2 in the US App Store in under 24 hours — a remarkable commercial reversal triggered entirely by Anthropic's safety stance.

## Key Data Points

- Anthropic had a $200 million existing contract with the US military before the dispute
- ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% after OpenAI announced its Pentagon deal
- Claude rose from outside the US App Store top 100 to #2 within 24 hours of the dispute going public
- The DoD/Trump directive gives Anthropic a 6-month wind-down period — but no formal supply-chain risk designation has been executed yet
- A managing partner at J2 Ventures told CNBC that 10 of his portfolio companies are actively replacing Claude for defense use cases
- Lockheed Martin and other contractors are already swapping out Anthropic models per Reuters
- A Washington Post report confirmed Claude models are being used for "real-time targeting and target prioritization" in the Iran campaign, integrated with Palantir's Maven system
- Amodei's memo: "The main reason [OpenAI] accepted [the DoD deal] and we did not is that they cared about placating employees, and we actually cared about preventing abuses"
- Amodei predicted OpenAI's "spin/gaslighting" was "not working very well on the general public or the media"
- OpenAI's contract language: AI systems available for "all lawful purposes" — Anthropic's specific objection was to this framing since laws can change
