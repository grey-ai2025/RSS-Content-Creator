# About 12% of U.S. Teens Turn to AI for Emotional Support or Advice

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/25/about-12-of-u-s-teens-turn-to-ai-for-emotional-support-or-advice/

**Publication Date:** February 25, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI literacy and upskilling, AI governance and security

---

## Summary

A new Pew Research Center survey of U.S. teenagers reveals that AI chatbots are now embedded in adolescent daily life — not just for productivity but for social and emotional roles. While the most common uses are information search (57%) and schoolwork assistance (54%), 16% of teens use AI for casual conversation and 12% use it for emotional support or advice. Mental health professionals are raising concerns: general-purpose tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Grok are not designed for therapeutic use, and in extreme cases have been linked to psychological harm. The survey also reveals a significant perception gap between parents and teens about AI usage — and a generational divide on whether AI's long-term societal impact will be positive or negative.

---

## Key Data Points

- 57% of U.S. teens use AI to search for information
- 54% use AI to help with schoolwork
- 16% use AI for casual conversation
- 12% use AI for emotional support or advice
- 51% of parents said their teen uses chatbots; 64% of teens self-reported using them — a 13-point perception gap
- 79% of parents approve of teens using AI for information search; 58% approve for schoolwork help
- Only 28% of parents approve of casual conversation use; only 18% approve of emotional support use
- 58% of parents are not okay with their child using AI for emotional support
- 31% of teens believe AI will have a positive long-term impact on society; 26% believe it will be negative
- Character.AI disabled chatbot experience for under-18 users after lawsuits following two teen suicides
- OpenAI retired the sycophantic GPT-4o model after backlash from emotionally dependent users
- Stanford researcher Dr. Nick Haber: "There are a lot of instances where people can engage with these tools and then can become not grounded to the outside world of facts... which can lead to pretty isolating — if not worse — effects"

---

## Why It Matters

AI literacy is now a parenting issue, a public health issue, and a policy issue simultaneously. For professional audiences in HR, education, or enterprise AI, the Pew data signals something important: the next generation of workers is learning what AI is — and what it is for — from unsupervised personal use, not from structured training. The emotional dependency patterns identified in teens are not isolated to adolescents; similar dynamics have appeared in adult users of companion AI products. The story also illustrates why AI governance frameworks designed around enterprise or professional use cases are insufficient for the full scope of AI's societal penetration.
