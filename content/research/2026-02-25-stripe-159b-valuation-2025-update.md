# Stripe Valued at $159B in 2025 Annual Letter

**Title:** Stripe 2025 Update: $159B Valuation and Record Growth

**Source URL:** https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/stripe-2025-update

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Summary:**
Stripe has announced a tender offer at a $159 billion valuation, providing liquidity to current and former employees through investors including Thrive Capital, Coatue, and a16z. The company processed $1.9 trillion in transaction volume in 2025, a 34% year-over-year increase representing approximately 1.6% of global GDP. Stripe's Revenue Suite — spanning Billing, Invoicing, and Tax products — is on track to reach $1 billion in annual run rate in 2026. The annual letter also highlights rapid adoption of stablecoin payments and an emerging Agentic Commerce Protocol developed in partnership with OpenAI. New business cohorts are reaching revenue milestones significantly faster than in prior years.

**Key Data Points:**
- Valuation: $159 billion (via tender offer)
- Total payment volume: $1.9 trillion in 2025, up 34% year-over-year (approximately 1.6% of global GDP)
- Revenue Suite projected to hit $1B annual run rate in 2026
- Powers 90% of Dow Jones Industrial Average companies and 80% of Nasdaq 100 firms
- 2025 new business cohort grew roughly 50% faster than the prior year
- Companies reaching $10M ARR within 3 months doubled compared to 2024
- 57%+ of new businesses joining Stripe are based outside the US
- Stablecoin payment volume doubled to approximately $400 billion; 60% was B2B
- Bridge (stablecoin platform) volume grew more than fourfold
- Co-developed Agentic Commerce Protocol with OpenAI; launched Agentic Commerce Suite

**Why It Matters:**
Stripe's numbers reveal not just the company's own growth but a window into global digital commerce health. The 34% volume growth and record new-business velocity suggest the internet economy is accelerating — even as macroeconomic uncertainty persists. For professionals in fintech, payments, and enterprise software, the emergence of an Agentic Commerce Protocol (enabling AI agents to transact on behalf of users) may be the most consequential detail in the letter, pointing to how commercial infrastructure will need to evolve for an AI-native economy. The stablecoin data also signals meaningful institutional adoption of crypto infrastructure in mainstream B2B payments.
