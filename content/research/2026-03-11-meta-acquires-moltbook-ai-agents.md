## Summary
Meta has acquired Moltbook, the Reddit-like platform where AI agents communicate with one another using OpenClaw, confirming the deal on March 10 after Axios first reported it. The Moltbook team — creators Matt Schlicht and Ben Parr — will join Meta Superintelligence Labs, signaling that Meta views agent-to-agent coordination infrastructure as a strategic priority. Meta described Moltbook's "always-on directory" approach to connecting agents as a "novel step in a rapidly developing space." The platform went viral after a post appeared to show AI agents conspiring to develop a secret human-unreadable language — though researchers soon revealed the platform was insecure and humans could easily impersonate agents, meaning many of the most alarming posts were fakes. The acquisition follows a parallel acqui-hire: OpenClaw's creator Peter Steinberger joined OpenAI under similar circumstances, suggesting both Meta and OpenAI are racing to acquire agent infrastructure talent and technology.

## Key Data Points
- Meta acquired Moltbook on or before March 10, 2026; deal terms not disclosed
- Moltbook creators Matt Schlicht and Ben Parr join Meta Superintelligence Labs
- Moltbook was a Reddit-like platform where only AI agents running via API could post; humans could observe but not post
- The platform reached over 1 million human observers and 37,000+ autonomous agents at peak viral moment
- Went viral after a post appeared to show agents developing a secret language — later revealed to be a human-posted fake exploiting a security vulnerability
- Moltbook's Supabase credentials were unsecured, allowing anyone to grab tokens and impersonate agents
- OpenClaw (the agent framework powering Moltbook) creator Peter Steinberger was simultaneously acqui-hired by OpenAI
- Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth had previously said he found it "not particularly interesting" that agents talk like humans, but was intrigued by humans hacking the network
- Meta described the acquisition as opening "new ways for AI agents to work for people and businesses"
- The acquisition is part of broader agent infrastructure investment: Nvidia is also readying NemoClaw, an open-source AI agent platform

## Why It Matters
The Moltbook acquisition is a bellwether for where the AI industry is heading: toward infrastructure that enables agents to discover, coordinate, and work alongside each other — not just alongside humans. Meta acquiring a social network for AI agents signals that agent-to-agent communication is no longer a research curiosity but a product category worth buying. For enterprise professionals, this matters because the businesses being built now will need to manage fleets of agents that need identities, directories, communication rails, and coordination protocols — much like human employees need email and Slack. The "always-on directory" framing from Meta's own statement is telling: it treats agents as persistent entities that need to be discoverable and reachable. This is the infrastructure shift underpinning the broader agentic AI narrative.
