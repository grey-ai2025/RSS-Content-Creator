# Anthropic Accuses Chinese AI Labs of Mining Claude Using 24,000 Fake Accounts

**Title:** Anthropic Accuses Chinese AI Labs of Mining Claude as US Debates AI Chip Exports

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/anthropic-accuses-chinese-ai-labs-of-mining-claude-as-us-debates-ai-chip-exports/

**Publication Date:** February 23, 2026

**Summary:**
Anthropic has accused three Chinese AI companies — DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — of systematically extracting Claude's capabilities using approximately 24,000 fake accounts in a technique known as "model distillation." The alleged scheme involved repeatedly querying Claude to capture its outputs and use them as training data to build competing models. The accusations emerge as US policymakers are actively debating export controls on AI chips designed to slow China's AI development. The exclusive TechCrunch report frames this as a major flashpoint in the ongoing US-China AI technology competition. Anthropic's disclosure adds evidence to concerns that frontier AI models are vulnerable to capability theft through adversarial querying at scale.

**Key Data Points:**
- Three Chinese AI labs accused: DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax
- Alleged use of approximately 24,000 fake accounts to distill Claude's capabilities
- Technique used: "model distillation" — using a frontier model's outputs to train a competing model
- Timing coincides with active US Congressional debate on AI chip export controls
- Reported as an exclusive investigation by TechCrunch on February 23, 2026

**Why It Matters:**
This story sits at the intersection of AI security, intellectual property, and geopolitics — all topics with profound implications for technology professionals. It raises urgent questions about how AI companies protect proprietary model capabilities from systematic extraction. For enterprise leaders, it highlights that AI models themselves are targets, not just data or code. For policymakers and compliance teams, it adds weight to arguments for stricter AI export controls.
