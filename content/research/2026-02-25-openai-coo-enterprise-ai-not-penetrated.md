# OpenAI COO: Enterprise AI Adoption Is Still Mostly Hype

**Title:** OpenAI COO Says "We Have Not Yet Really Seen AI Penetrate Enterprise Business Processes"

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/openai-coo-says-we-have-not-yet-really-seen-ai-penetrate-enterprise-business-processes/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Summary:**
OpenAI COO Brad Lightcap delivered a candid assessment that AI has not yet meaningfully transformed core enterprise business processes, despite months of industry headlines predicting the death of SaaS and the rise of autonomous AI agents replacing enterprise software. Lightcap acknowledged that while predictions about AI disrupting enterprise software have occasionally moved stock prices, actual deep integration of AI into business workflows remains limited. The comments reflect a broader moment of market recalibration as enterprise buyers move from AI experimentation to demanding real, measurable ROI. Lightcap's remarks are particularly notable coming from the COO of the company most synonymous with the AI boom — and underscore the gap between AI hype cycles and ground-level enterprise deployment realities.

**Key Data Points:**
- Brad Lightcap is COO of OpenAI, the company behind ChatGPT and the GPT-4 model family
- Lightcap stated: "We have not yet really seen AI penetrate enterprise business processes"
- He noted that "SaaS is dead" predictions have influenced stock markets but haven't translated to real operational change
- Comments came amid a wave of AI agent product launches from Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, and Salesforce
- The remarks align with Fast Company's Feb 24 piece "What if the SaaSpocalypse is a myth?"
- Enterprise AI adoption remains a major theme for Feb 2026, with IBM, Google Cloud, and Anthropic all competing for enterprise AI spend

**Why It Matters:**
When OpenAI's own COO says enterprise AI adoption hasn't really happened yet, it's a signal worth paying attention to. For technology leaders, vendors, and investors, this is a grounding data point in a market flooded with AI product announcements. It suggests that the enterprise AI opportunity remains largely untapped — which is either a bullish sign for the sector's long-term growth or a warning that AI tools are not yet delivering the promised workflow transformation. Either way, it sets a clear benchmark: the next chapter of AI in business is about proving ROI, not just demonstrating capability.
