# Gemini Can Now Execute Multi-Step Tasks on Android — Agentic AI Hits the Consumer Layer

**Title:** Gemini can now automate some multi-step tasks on Android

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/25/gemini-can-now-automate-some-multi-step-tasks-on-android/

**Publication Date:** February 25, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI agents, agentic AI

## Summary

Google has expanded Gemini's capabilities on Android to include autonomous execution of multi-step tasks — meaning users can instruct the assistant to complete sequences of actions across apps, not just answer questions. Initial capabilities include requesting rideshare pickups and placing food delivery orders, with the assistant navigating the required app interactions on the user's behalf. This is a meaningful milestone: agentic AI has moved from enterprise server infrastructure into the consumer device already in billions of people's pockets. The expansion marks a direct advance in Google's effort to transform Gemini from a conversational assistant into an autonomous agent capable of operating the phone itself. It puts Google in direct competition with Apple Intelligence's action-oriented features and signals that the race to own the consumer AI agent layer is accelerating.

## Key Data Points

- Gemini can now autonomously complete multi-step tasks on Android, including rideshare and food delivery requests
- The agent navigates across applications independently to complete the assigned task
- This is a consumer-facing deployment of agentic AI — previously concentrated in enterprise and developer contexts
- Competing consumer agent efforts: Apple Intelligence (action capabilities), OpenAI's Operator (web-based), Samsung's Galaxy AI
- Android has approximately 3.6 billion active users globally — the scale of this deployment is significant
- Google is simultaneously deploying agentic features across Workspace (Docs, Gmail, Sheets) and Android consumer devices

## Why It Matters

Consumer-level AI agents are where agentic AI stops being an enterprise IT conversation and becomes a mass-market behavioral shift. Gemini executing tasks autonomously on Android — on devices already in people's hands, with no additional hardware or subscription required — is a different category of deployment than enterprise agent pilots. It normalizes the idea of AI acting on your behalf across apps as a default smartphone behavior. For professionals tracking AI adoption curves, this is the signal that agentic AI has crossed from early-adopter infrastructure into mainstream consumer product. The question now is not whether people will use AI agents — it is which platform will own that relationship.
