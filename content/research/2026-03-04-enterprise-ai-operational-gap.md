# Bridging the Operational AI Gap: Survey of 500 IT Leaders Reveals What Separates AI Success From Failure

**Source URL:** https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/03/04/1133642/bridging-the-operational-ai-gap/

**Publication Date:** March 4, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI agents as enterprise infrastructure (Topic 3); "from experimentation to execution" framing; context engineering / MCP as integration layer (Topic 6); "agents are infrastructure now"

---

## Summary

MIT Technology Review Insights, in partnership with Celigo, surveyed 500 senior IT leaders at mid-to-large US companies pursuing AI to understand what separates successful AI deployments from failed pilots. The survey, conducted in December 2025, finds that three in four companies now have at least one department with an AI workflow fully in production — but two-thirds still lack dedicated AI teams, and the gap between departmental pilots and enterprise-wide deployment remains wide. The research identifies a clear differentiator: companies with enterprise-wide integration platforms are five times more likely to use diverse data sources in AI workflows and are significantly more advanced in multi-departmental AI adoption and autonomous workflow execution. Gartner separately predicts over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027 due to cost, inaccuracy, and governance failures — and the survey suggests the missing ingredient is not the AI itself, but the operational foundation underneath it.

---

## Key Data Points

- **76% of surveyed companies** have at least one department with an AI workflow fully in production (as of December 2025)
- **43% of organizations** find AI most successful when applied to **well-defined, established processes**; 25% succeed with new processes; 32% apply AI to various processes
- **Only 34% of organizations** have a dedicated team for maintaining AI workflows — the remaining 66% distribute responsibility across central IT (21%), departmental operations (25%), or spread it across functions (19%)
- Companies with enterprise-wide integration platforms are **5x more likely to use 5+ diverse data sources** in AI workflows (59% vs. 11% for workflow-specific integrations, vs. 0% for no integration platform)
- **Gartner prediction: over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027** due to cost, inaccuracy, and governance challenges
- Organizations using enterprise-wide integration platforms show more multi-departmental AI implementation, more workflow autonomy, and greater confidence in expanding AI autonomy
- The core thesis: "The real issue is not the AI itself, but the missing operational foundation"
- Survey methodology: 500 senior IT leaders at mid-to-large US companies, all actively pursuing AI, surveyed December 2025

---

## Why It Matters

This survey provides the clearest empirical snapshot yet of where enterprise AI actually stands heading into 2026. The 76% in-production figure sounds optimistic, but the 40% Gartner cancellation forecast and the 66% without dedicated AI teams reveal the fragility underneath. For professionals responsible for AI strategy, the data makes a specific structural argument: integration infrastructure — not model selection — is the bottleneck. Organizations that treat AI deployment as a model procurement decision, without solving for data integration and governance, are building on sand. The five-times multiplier for diverse data source usage is particularly significant for anyone designing AI agent workflows, where access to varied, real-time data sources is the difference between a useful agent and a hallucinating one.

## Tips/How-To Angle

This story is purpose-built for a **"5 things to get right before scaling AI across your organization"** framework. Actionable angles:

- **Start with well-defined processes:** 43% of organizations succeed by applying AI to already-automated, established workflows — not by trying to automate chaotic ones first
- **Build a dedicated AI ops function:** Two-thirds of companies don't have one — the ones that do are measurably more advanced; treat AI maintenance like infrastructure, not a project
- **Invest in integration before investing in models:** Five times more data source diversity comes from having an enterprise integration layer — this is the unlock for more capable AI agents
- **Quantify governance before scaling autonomy:** Gartner's 40% cancellation forecast is almost entirely driven by cost, inaccuracy, and governance failures — all preventable with upfront design
- **Don't conflate departmental success with enterprise readiness:** Winning a pilot in one department does not mean the integration and governance infrastructure exists to scale — that gap is where most AI initiatives stall
