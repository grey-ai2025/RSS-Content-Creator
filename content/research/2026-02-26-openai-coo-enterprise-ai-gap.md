# OpenAI COO: 'We Have Not Yet Really Seen AI Penetrate Enterprise Business Processes'

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/openai-coo-says-we-have-not-yet-really-seen-ai-penetrate-enterprise-business-processes/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI in the enterprise, agentic AI, AI agents, AI job market and workforce transformation

---

## Summary

OpenAI COO Brad Lightcap stated publicly that despite massive AI investment and billions in revenue, enterprise AI adoption has not yet reached business processes at scale. Speaking at the India AI Impact Summit in New Delhi, Lightcap described enterprises as "highly complex organizations" where AI has not yet bridged the gap from individual productivity tools to systemic workflow integration. OpenAI launched its new Frontier platform for enterprise agent management in response to this gap, and simultaneously announced partnerships with major consultancies including BCG, McKinsey, Accenture, and Capgemini to drive enterprise deployment. Lightcap signaled a shift in how OpenAI will measure success: by business outcomes, not seat licenses.

---

## Key Data Points

- OpenAI ended 2025 with over $20 billion in annualized revenue
- India is the second-largest ChatGPT user base outside the U.S., with over 100 million weekly users
- OpenAI partnered with BCG, McKinsey, Accenture, and Capgemini days after Lightcap's statement to push enterprise deployment
- Lightcap acknowledged OpenAI itself was "a massive Slack user last year" — still relying on traditional enterprise software
- OpenAI Frontier pricing not yet announced; success will be measured by "business outcomes, not seat licenses"
- India is only 4th in enterprise AI seats in Asia — below its scale as a market
- Lightcap: "We almost always find ourselves having to manage too much demand"
- Indian IT stocks dipped following Anthropic's COBOL announcement as markets priced in automation risk for IT services and BPO sectors

---

## Why It Matters

The most direct admission yet from the world's leading AI company that the gap between AI hype and enterprise reality remains wide. When OpenAI's COO says AI hasn't truly penetrated enterprise business processes — while simultaneously reporting $20B in revenue — it crystallizes the distinction between individual AI adoption and systemic transformation. For professional audiences, this is the most authoritative framing of "the gap between hype and production" available. It also signals that the next wave of AI competition is not about models — it is about workflow integration and organizational change management.
