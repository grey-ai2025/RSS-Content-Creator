# Meta Strikes Up to $100B AMD Chip Deal as It Chases 'Personal Superintelligence'

**Title:** Meta Strikes Up to $100B AMD Chip Deal as It Chases 'Personal Superintelligence'

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/meta-strikes-up-to-100b-amd-chip-deal-as-it-chases-personal-superintelligence/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Summary:**
Meta has secured a multiyear agreement to purchase up to $100 billion in AMD artificial intelligence chips, marking a major strategic move to reduce its dependence on Nvidia and expand data center capacity. The deal includes a 160 million-share warrant component, directly tying AMD's equity upside to Meta's procurement commitment. This move is framed under Meta's stated ambition to develop "personal superintelligence," signaling the company's long-term AI infrastructure vision. The agreement reflects the broader industry trend of hyperscalers locking in long-term chip supply deals to manage cost and supply chain risk. It also represents a significant vote of confidence in AMD as a credible alternative to Nvidia in the high-end AI chip market.

**Key Data Points:**
- Deal value: up to $100 billion in AMD AI chips over multiple years
- Includes a 160 million-share warrant tied to Meta's chip procurement
- Strategic goal: diversify beyond Nvidia and scale data center capacity
- Framed under Meta's "personal superintelligence" AI initiative
- AMD is positioned as a primary alternative supplier for large-scale AI compute

**Why It Matters:**
This deal signals a structural shift in the AI chip supply chain. For business and technology professionals, it underscores how critical semiconductor procurement strategy has become for companies competing in AI. Meta's willingness to commit up to $100 billion to a single alternative supplier demonstrates how heavily capital is flowing into AI infrastructure — and how seriously large tech companies are treating supply chain diversification as a strategic risk mitigation priority. AMD's rising status as a Nvidia alternative also has broad implications for chip market dynamics and enterprise procurement strategies.
