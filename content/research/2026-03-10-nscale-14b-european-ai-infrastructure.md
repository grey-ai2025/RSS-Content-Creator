## Summary
British AI infrastructure startup Nscale closed a $2 billion Series C — described as "the largest in European history" — bringing its valuation to $14.6 billion and positioning it as one of Europe's most significant AI decacorns. The raise was supported by Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan, widely read as IPO preparation, with CEO Josh Payne telling the New York Times the company may go public "as early as this year." Former Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg, former Yahoo president Susan Decker, and former UK Deputy Prime Minister Nick Clegg are joining the board, signaling a deliberate push toward institutional credibility and geopolitical positioning. Nscale's "Stargate Norway" joint venture with Aker, targeting 100,000 Nvidia GPUs by end of 2026 with OpenAI as an anchor customer, will now be fully managed by Nscale. The company has also signed an expanded Microsoft deal covering approximately 200,000 Nvidia GPUs across three European data centers and one in the US.

## Key Data Points
- $2 billion Series C — Nscale describes it as "the largest in European history"
- $14.6 billion post-money valuation, placing Nscale alongside Helsing and Mistral AI as European decacorns
- Investors include Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Nvidia, Dell, Citadel, Jane Street, Nokia, Point72, Lenovo, Astra Capital, and Blue Owl
- $1.4 billion delayed draw term loan backed by GPUs also raised last month
- Stargate Norway targeting 100,000 Nvidia GPUs by end of 2026; OpenAI is an initial customer
- Microsoft deal covers approximately 200,000 Nvidia GPUs across European and US data centers
- Board additions: Sheryl Sandberg (former Meta COO), Susan Decker (former Yahoo president), Nick Clegg (former UK Deputy PM and former Meta VP of Global Affairs)
- IPO potentially as early as 2026 according to CEO Josh Payne
- CEO Josh Payne previously worked in coal mining before pivoting to AI infrastructure

## Why It Matters
Nscale represents the clearest signal yet that European AI infrastructure is consolidating into serious institutional capital — not just venture bets. The board composition (Sandberg, Clegg) is a deliberate statement: this is about geopolitics as much as compute. With OpenAI as a customer and Microsoft as a strategic partner, Nscale is effectively building the European layer of what could become a bifurcated global AI infrastructure, separate from US hyperscalers. For enterprise leaders evaluating AI infrastructure strategy, the question of where compute lives — and under what legal and regulatory jurisdiction — is no longer abstract. The Stargate Norway model (renewable energy, local governance, NATO-aligned) is a direct response to concerns about concentration of AI infrastructure in US-controlled entities.
