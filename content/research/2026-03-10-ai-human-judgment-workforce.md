# Why AI Flooding the Market Makes Human Judgment More Valuable, Not Less

**Title:** Why AI makes human judgment more valuable

**Source URL:** https://www.fastcompany.com/91505544/why-ai-makes-human-judgment-more-valuable

**Publication Date:** March 9, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI workforce transformation; AI agents in enterprise; "the future of work"

**Summary:** A Fast Company essay by Jae Lee argues that AI is most effective when it operates "middle-to-middle" — augmenting human decision-making in the messy middle of workflows — rather than end-to-end autonomy. The core thesis is that as AI handles more of the routine, pattern-matching, and execution work, the premium on distinctly human capabilities — contextual judgment, ethical reasoning, ambiguity resolution, and stakeholder navigation — rises sharply. Rather than making human workers redundant, AI's expansion of capability creates a new scarcity: humans who can effectively direct, evaluate, and take accountability for AI outputs. The piece positions this as a case for deliberate skill development, not passive reassurance.

**Key Data Points:**
- Argument framing: AI is "middle-to-middle, not end-to-end" — meaning it handles the middle execution layers while humans remain critical at decision entry and accountability exit
- As AI generates more outputs at scale, the ability to evaluate, select, and stake accountability for those outputs becomes more valuable
- The piece argues that judgment — the ability to navigate ambiguity, weigh competing values, and act in contexts that AI cannot fully model — becomes the scarce resource in an AI-saturated labor market
- Fast Company Impact Council contributor Jae Lee frames this as a strategic imperative for professional development
- The thesis connects to LinkedIn's own 2026 data showing 1.3M new AI-enabled jobs created and 92% YoY increase in AI learning time — with the implication that the highest-value work requires human oversight of AI outputs
- Implicit data point: as AI commoditizes execution, differentiation migrates to judgment-layer roles

**Why It Matters:** The AI workforce narrative has been dominated by displacement anxiety. This piece offers the complementary argument: that scarcity and value track together, and if AI is commoditizing execution, judgment becomes the premium asset. For professional audiences, this reframes career development strategy — the question is no longer "will AI take my job?" but "am I investing in the judgment-layer skills that AI cannot replicate?" This is a high-resonance argument for LinkedIn's professional audience precisely because it provides a concrete answer to a question everyone is asking.

**Related Themes:** AI workforce transformation, future of work, human-AI collaboration, professional development, AI augmentation, enterprise AI, AI skills premium
