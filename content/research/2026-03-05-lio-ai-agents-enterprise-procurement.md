# Lio Raises $30M from Andreessen Horowitz to Replace Enterprise Procurement Teams With AI Agents

## Summary

Lio, a startup building AI agents that autonomously execute the entire enterprise procurement workflow, announced a $30 million Series A led by Andreessen Horowitz today. The company's thesis is a direct challenge to legacy procurement software vendors like SAP Ariba and Oracle: rather than building tools to help humans do procurement faster, Lio deploys agents that execute the workflow themselves — reading documents, evaluating suppliers, negotiating terms, and completing transactions without human intervention in the loop.

CEO Vladimir Keil's framing is precise and commercially significant: "Every previous generation of procurement technology was built on the same assumption — that humans will do the work and technology will help them do it faster. We take a fundamentally different approach." This is the operating model of the emerging agentic AI category applied to one of enterprise's most manual, fragmented back-office functions. Procurement touches every dollar a company spends, yet it remains largely reliant on ERP software, email chains, and manual compliance checks — exactly the kind of unstructured, repetitive workflow AI agents are built for.

The round included SV Angels, Harry Stebbings, and YC (Lio was part of the Spring 2023 batch), bringing total funding to $33 million. The company is already managing billions in enterprise spend across clients, with one global manufacturer automating 75% of previously outsourced procurement operations within six months of deployment.

## Key Data Points

- $30 million Series A led by Andreessen Horowitz; $33 million total funding raised
- Also backed by SV Angels, Harry Stebbings, YC (Spring 2023 batch)
- Lio's agents operate across ERP systems, contract management tools, supplier databases, and compliance workflows
- One client (a global manufacturer) automated 75% of its previously outsourced procurement operations within 6 months
- Keil: "Processes that once took weeks can now be completed in minutes"
- Company is already managing "billions in enterprise spend"
- Competitors displaced: SAP Ariba, Oracle procurement, BPO providers, and consulting firms that handle procurement outsourcing
- Capital will be used to expand US footprint and increase agent capabilities
- Lio was founded in 2023 by three co-founders with firsthand experience of broken enterprise procurement as both buyer and seller

## Relevance

Lio is a clean, concrete instantiation of the SaaS disruption thesis that's dominating enterprise AI conversation in 2026. The story is useful not because procurement is inherently interesting, but because the model — agents replacing software plus the humans that operate it — is the architecture being deployed across every enterprise function. For LinkedIn's professional audience, this story answers a specific question: what does "agentic AI replacing software" actually look like in practice? The answer is: it looks like a startup that just raised $30M from a16z to automate the procurement department. The Gartner estimate that 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027 makes this funding story more interesting, not less — Lio is explicitly building the integration infrastructure to avoid that failure mode.
