# Research: Jack Dorsey Explains the Block Layoffs — Rebuilding as "an Intelligence"

**Title:** Jack Dorsey Is Ready to Explain the Block Layoffs

**Source URL:** https://www.wired.com/story/jack-dorsey-explains-block-layoffs/

**Publication Date:** March 6, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI-driven layoffs and workforce displacement (#4); AI agents — enterprise adoption (#2)

---

## Summary

Jack Dorsey gave his first in-depth interview following Block's decision to cut nearly 4,000 employees — approximately 40% of its workforce. Dorsey frames the move not as a correction to overhiring but as a deliberate structural response to a fundamental shift in what AI tools can now do. He says something decisive changed in December 2025, when Anthropic's Opus 4.6 and OpenAI's Codex 5.3 became capable of working with large, complex codebases — not just greenfield projects. That shift, he argues, changes the viable structure of any company.

Dorsey's stated vision is to rebuild Block as "a mini AGI" — a company organized around an intelligence layer that employees, and eventually customers, can query and build on top of. He explicitly rejects the inherited management hierarchy of the 20th century as a constraint on what AI-native organizations can become. His framing is clear: companies that don't restructure around AI in the next one to two years face an existential threat from irrelevance.

The interview is notable for its candor. Dorsey does not deny that other CEOs may see this as a template. He states plainly that Block's metric (gross profit per employee) was already ahead of peers — meaning this was not a correction to bloat but a proactive restructuring anticipating a new technological reality. He also says he believes workers will find new roles, but acknowledges the company's structure will be "markedly different."

Dorsey draws a sharp distinction between Musk's Twitter layoffs (financially motivated, debt-driven) and Block's cuts (strategically motivated, technology-driven). He also gestures toward OpenClaw as evidence that people still want decentralized agency, and weighs in briefly on the Anthropic/Pentagon standoff — expressing support for Anthropic's principled stand.

---

## Key Data Points

- Block employs approximately 10,000 people; roughly 4,000 were laid off (40% of workforce)
- Block made nearly $3 billion in gross profit last quarter; market cap is $39 billion
- Dorsey cites December 2025 as the inflection point when Anthropic Opus 4.6 and OpenAI Codex 5.3 became capable of working on large, complex codebases
- Block's gross profit per employee was "exactly in line with or just ahead of all peers" before the layoffs — ruling out overhiring as the primary driver
- Dorsey's vision: no management hierarchy, an intelligence layer every employee can query, customers vibe-coding personalized products on top of Block's platform
- "Every company that's not building itself as intelligence is going to face something existential, and it's going to happen over the next year or two"
- Dorsey: "If I were to build a company today I would just do it in a completely different way. I would have no management hierarchy whatsoever."
- Quote on the AI shift: "Something really shifted in December in the sophistication of tools. It presented an option to dramatically change how any company is structured."

---

## Why It Matters

This is the most explicit CEO-level articulation yet of the business logic behind AI-driven workforce restructuring. Dorsey is not hedging. He is stating that the company-as-intelligence model is inevitable, and that the only question is whether companies get ahead of it or react too late. For a professional LinkedIn audience, this is the concrete, named, data-backed version of the "future of work" conversation that has been mostly theoretical. A $39B company with strong financials restructuring proactively — not reactively — is a qualitatively different signal than a struggling company using AI as cover for cost cuts. Dorsey's framing also directly addresses the "AI-washing layoffs" critique, which gives the story more credibility and nuance.
