# AI Investor Loyalty Is (Almost) Dead: A Dozen OpenAI VCs Now Also Back Anthropic

**Title:** With AI, Investor Loyalty Is (Almost) Dead: At Least a Dozen OpenAI VCs Now Also Back Anthropic

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/23/with-ai-investor-loyalty-is-almost-dead-at-least-a-dozen-openai-vcs-now-also-back-anthropic/

**Publication Date:** February 23, 2026

**Summary:**
A TechCrunch investigation has found that at least a dozen venture capital firms have invested in both OpenAI and Anthropic — direct competitors — breaking longstanding industry norms around conflict-of-interest ethics in venture investing. Named firms with dual positions include Founders Fund and ICONIQ Capital. Historically, venture firms avoided co-investing in competing companies due to concerns about divided loyalties and compromised board-level advice. The article argues that the AI boom has effectively dissolved these conventions, with capital abundance and uncertainty about which lab will "win" driving portfolio hedging behavior. The piece frames this as both a market signal — investors are unsure who will dominate — and an ethical problem that the VC industry has not formally addressed.

**Key Data Points:**
- At least 12 venture capital firms identified as investors in both OpenAI and Anthropic
- Named firms include Founders Fund and ICONIQ Capital
- Described as "the disregard of a longstanding ethical conflict-of-interest rule"
- Some dual investments described as "shocking" by the authors
- Trend reflects broader uncertainty about which AI lab will ultimately prevail
- TechCrunch describes some dual investors as "understandable" and others as clearly problematic

**Why It Matters:**
For founders, limited partners, and enterprise decision-makers, this story raises important questions about the integrity of venture capital governance during the AI supercycle. When a board member's firm holds stakes in a direct competitor, the quality of strategic advice — and the security of confidential information — is compromised. For business leaders evaluating AI partnerships or investment, understanding the financial web connecting these labs matters. More broadly, this trend reflects how extraordinary the AI moment is: even experienced investors are abandoning established risk frameworks in pursuit of exposure.
