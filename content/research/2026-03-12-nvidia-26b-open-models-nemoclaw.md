# Nvidia Bets $26 Billion on Open-Weight AI Models — and Launches Its Own Agent Platform

**Title:** Nvidia Will Spend $26 Billion to Build Open-Weight AI Models, Filings Show + Nvidia Planning Open-Source OpenClaw Competitor (NemoClaw)

**Source URL:** https://www.wired.com/story/nvidia-investing-26-billion-open-source-models/

**Secondary Source:** https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/03/nvidia-is-reportedly-planning-its-own-open-source-openclaw-competitor/

**Publication Date:** March 11, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI agents, edge AI / smaller models, agentic AI

**Summary:**
Nvidia is making a decisive move beyond chipmaker status: the company will invest $26 billion over five years to build open-weight AI frontier models, according to SEC filings confirmed by executives in interviews. Simultaneously, Nvidia is preparing to launch NemoClaw, its own open-source AI agent platform designed to compete directly with OpenClaw — the "always-on" agent tool that Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang called "the most important software release probably ever." Nvidia has already released Nemotron 3 Super, a 128-billion parameter open-weight model it claims outperforms GPT-OSS on multiple benchmarks. NemoClaw is being pitched to corporate partners including Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike ahead of Nvidia's annual developer conference.

**Key Data Points:**
- $26 billion committed over five years to open-weight frontier model development (confirmed in 2025 SEC filings)
- Nemotron 3 Super has 128 billion parameters; Nvidia claims it scores 37 on the AI Index vs. GPT-OSS's 33
- Nvidia has already completed pretraining of a 550-billion-parameter model
- NemoClaw will include "security and privacy tools" — directly addressing the enterprise concerns around OpenClaw's permissive data access
- Partners being pitched include Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike
- NemoClaw will run on machines without Nvidia's own GPUs — but Nvidia's GPU dominance means it benefits from any agentic AI workload expansion
- Nvidia VP Bryan Catanzaro: "Nvidia is taking open model development much more seriously. And we are making a lot of progress."
- The move counters Chinese open models (DeepSeek, Alibaba Qwen) that are attracting global developers away from US-made alternatives

**Why It Matters:**
Nvidia is no longer content to be the infrastructure layer — it is becoming a full-stack AI company. The $26B open-model commitment gives enterprises and developers an American-built alternative to the Chinese open models that currently dominate the open-source landscape. NemoClaw is equally significant: by building an enterprise-grade AI agent platform with native security controls, Nvidia is positioning itself at the orchestration layer of agentic AI — the exact layer where future competitive moats will be built. For enterprise technology leaders, this changes the procurement conversation from "which GPU?" to "which agent platform?"
