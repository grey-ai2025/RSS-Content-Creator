# OpenAI Acquires Promptfoo to Harden Its AI Agent Platform Against Adversarial Attacks

**Title:** OpenAI acquires Promptfoo to secure its AI agents

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/openai-acquires-promptfoo-to-secure-its-ai-agents/

**Publication Date:** March 9, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI cyber risk; Agentic AI; AI agents in enterprise

**Summary:** OpenAI announced on March 9 the acquisition of Promptfoo, a two-year-old AI security startup that builds tools for testing LLM vulnerabilities. Promptfoo's technology will be integrated into OpenAI Frontier, the company's enterprise platform for autonomous AI agents. The deal signals that frontier AI labs are now treating agent security as a prerequisite for enterprise deployment — not an afterthought. Promptfoo's tools will enable automated red-teaming of agentic workflows, continuous monitoring for compliance risks, and security evaluation of agent behavior before and after deployment. The acquisition was not priced publicly; Promptfoo had raised just $23 million total and was valued at $86 million as of July 2025.

**Key Data Points:**
- Promptfoo founded in 2024 by Ian Webster and Michael D'Angelo
- Raised $23 million total; last valuation $86 million (July 2025, per PitchBook)
- Promptfoo's products used by more than 25% of Fortune 500 companies
- Technology being integrated into OpenAI Frontier (enterprise AI agent platform)
- Capabilities: automated red-teaming, agentic workflow security evaluation, runtime activity monitoring for risks and compliance
- Open source Promptfoo library and interface will continue to be developed post-acquisition
- Acquisition price not disclosed
- Context: the deal comes as autonomous AI agents taking on digital tasks create new vectors for bad actors to access sensitive data or manipulate automated systems

**Why It Matters:** As AI agents gain access to file systems, email inboxes, calendars, and enterprise APIs, the attack surface grows dramatically. The fact that a frontier lab is now embedding automated red-teaming directly into its agent platform — before enterprises even ask — is a meaningful signal. Security is no longer an enterprise add-on; it's becoming table stakes for agentic AI products. Enterprise buyers evaluating agent platforms in 2026 should treat native security tooling as a baseline expectation, not a differentiator.

**Related Themes:** AI cyber risk, agentic AI, enterprise AI security, LLM security, red-teaming, AI agents, OpenAI Frontier, AI governance
