# Waymo Robotaxis Now Operating in 10 US Cities

**Title:** Waymo Robotaxis Are Now Operating in 10 US Cities

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/waymo-robotaxis-are-now-operating-in-10-us-cities/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Summary:**
Waymo, the autonomous vehicle subsidiary of Alphabet, has expanded its robotaxi service to four new US cities — Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Orlando — bringing its total operating footprint to 10 cities. As with previous launches, initial access is limited to invited riders who have the Waymo app installed. The company's vehicles are equipped with 29 cameras, five lidars, and six radar trackers capable of 360-degree environmental detection. This expansion marks a significant acceleration in Waymo's commercial rollout strategy, moving beyond its original West Coast markets into major Sun Belt metros. The move further establishes Waymo as the leading deployed autonomous vehicle service in the United States.

**Key Data Points:**
- Now operating in 10 US cities total
- Four new cities added simultaneously: Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Orlando
- Initial access via invitation through the Waymo app
- Vehicle hardware: 29 cameras, five lidars, six radar trackers (360-degree detection)
- Waymo is owned by Alphabet (Google's parent company)
- Follows an established playbook of controlled geographic rollout with invited riders

**Why It Matters:**
Waymo's simultaneous launch in four new cities signals that autonomous vehicle technology is crossing from pilot phase into structured commercial scaling. For transportation, logistics, and urban planning professionals, this expansion marks a meaningful milestone. For investors and entrepreneurs, Waymo's growing footprint raises competitive pressure on rideshare incumbents like Uber and Lyft, which are also pursuing autonomous solutions. The choice of major Sun Belt cities — with their grid-based streets and favorable weather — also reflects a deliberate market prioritization strategy worth studying.
