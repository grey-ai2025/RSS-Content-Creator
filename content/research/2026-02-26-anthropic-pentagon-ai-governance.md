# Anthropic Won't Budge as Pentagon Escalates AI Dispute

**Source URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-wont-budge-as-pentagon-escalates-ai-dispute/

**Publication Date:** February 24, 2026

**Keyword Match:** AI governance and security, agentic AI, AI in the enterprise

---

## Summary

The U.S. Department of Defense gave Anthropic until Friday, February 27 to grant unrestricted military access to its Claude AI model — or face being designated a "supply chain risk," a classification normally reserved for foreign adversaries. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth summoned Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei to Washington and threatened to invoke the Defense Production Act to force compliance. Anthropic is refusing to compromise on its policies against using Claude for mass surveillance of Americans or fully autonomous weapons. The dispute is the most consequential AI governance confrontation in U.S. history, with implications for every AI company operating under government contracts.

---

## Key Data Points

- Pentagon deadline: Friday, February 27, 2026 — agree to "any lawful use" terms or face consequences
- Potential action: Declaring Anthropic a "supply chain risk" (a designation typically reserved for foreign adversaries like Chinese tech companies) or invoking the Defense Production Act
- Anthropic is the only frontier AI lab with classified DOD access; the Pentagon has no backup plan
- DOD reportedly struck a side deal with Elon Musk's xAI to use Grok in classified systems — but this is not yet an operational replacement
- Dean Ball, Foundation for American Innovation: "If Anthropic canceled the contract tomorrow, it would be a serious problem for the DOD"
- OpenAI and xAI have already agreed to the "any lawful use" terms that Anthropic is refusing
- Anthropic's valuation: approximately $380 billion
- The Defense Production Act was last invoked during COVID-19 to compel GM and 3M to manufacture medical equipment

---

## Why It Matters

This story redefines the AI governance conversation from a regulatory abstraction to a live political conflict. A government threatening to invoke emergency wartime powers to override an AI company's safety policies is unprecedented. For professional audiences — especially those in enterprise AI, legal, compliance, or defense-adjacent sectors — this signals that AI governance is no longer a compliance checkbox. It is a commercial risk that can threaten a company's entire government business. The outcome of this dispute will set a precedent for how AI safety policies interact with government procurement for years.
