$100 billion on chips. That's not a budget — that's a threat.

Meta just inked a multiyear deal to buy up to $100B in AMD AI chips, complete with a 160 million-share warrant that ties AMD's stock upside directly to Meta's procurement. If that's not a supplier relationship, that's a marriage.

The stated goal? "Personal superintelligence." Which sounds wild until you realize Meta has 3+ billion daily active users and needs serious compute to make that dream even vaguely plausible.

The real subtext here is Nvidia. Meta is the latest hyperscaler making it clear they don't want to be a one-supplier shop when that supplier can basically name its price. Diversifying to AMD is less about AMD being better and more about not having Nvidia's phone number be the most important contact in your CFO's Rolodex.

For the rest of us watching from the sidelines: when a single company commits $100B to chips — not products, not services, just raw compute — it tells you everything about where the real AI arms race is being fought. Not in models. In data centers.

AMD went from "the other chip company" to "we take warrants now." Glow-up of the decade.
