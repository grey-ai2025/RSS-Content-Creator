## HEADLINE
More than 20 AI-powered wartime intelligence dashboards — many built in days using vibe coding tools — are spreading AI-generated summaries and fake satellite imagery of an active conflict while giving users the feeling of being informed.

## KEY FACTS
- Digital investigations expert has logged 20+ AI-powered dashboards aggregating satellite data, ship tracking, AI news summaries, and prediction market links in real time for the Iran conflict
- Fake AI-generated satellite imagery of the conflict is already spreading online — eroding trust in satellite evidence, which is typically considered the gold standard of conflict verification
- Quote: "There's an illusion of being on top of things and being in control, where all you're really doing is pulling in a ton of signals and not necessarily understanding what you're seeing"
- Source credibility: Named digital investigations researcher; Financial Times fake imagery finding; Palantir/DoD deployment confirmed

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the AI literacy problem that doesn't show up in enterprise pilot reviews: AI can manufacture the *feeling* of expertise without delivering it. The same capability that makes AI useful for knowledge synthesis — rapid aggregation of complex signals — becomes actively dangerous when the user lacks the domain expertise to evaluate what the synthesis is missing. For organizational leaders, this is the clearest case study for why AI literacy isn't about using the tools. It's about knowing what the tools cannot tell you.
