Your AI agent just confidently told you something that was true six months ago. Congrats on the very expensive, very stale advice.

Knowledge cutoffs are one of the most underappreciated blockers to real enterprise AI agent deployment. Most models know a lot — about the past. Real-time prices, regulatory changes, competitive shifts, live inventory data? That's where agents start hallucinating with confidence.

Nimble just raised $47M to fix exactly this.

Their platform uses AI agents to crawl the web, validate what they find, clean the data, and structure it into queryable tables — so downstream agents can actually work with current information. It's the plumbing layer the agentic AI stack has been quietly missing.

Think of it as giving your AI agents a live internet connection that doesn't lie to them.

For enterprise architects and CTOs building multi-agent systems, this is a legitimately important infrastructure piece. Agentic AI is only as good as the data it can act on. A reasoning engine with stale inputs is just a very confident wrong answer machine.

The $47M raise signals that investors get this. The next phase of AI agent investment isn't in the models — it's in the infrastructure that makes those models useful in production: real-time data, validation layers, and clean pipelines that agents can actually trust.

Smaller models, bigger impact — but only if the data underneath them is fresh.

The AI agent stack is growing up. One unglamorous infrastructure layer at a time.
