When OpenAI's own COO says enterprise AI hasn't really landed yet, maybe it's time to update the slide deck.

Brad Lightcap this week: "We have not yet really seen AI penetrate enterprise business processes."

This is OpenAI's COO. The company whose product set off the AI hype cycle. Saying, publicly, that the hype hasn't translated to ground-level enterprise transformation yet.

Respect the honesty.

Here's what most people are missing: this is actually a bullish signal disguised as a sobering one. If AI adoption in enterprise workflows is still in early innings, the opportunity ahead is massive. We're not at the end of the AI-in-business story — we're somewhere around chapter two.

But the tone is shifting. Enterprise buyers have moved from "let's run a pilot" to "show me the ROI." The "SaaS is dead" headlines have occasionally moved stock prices. They haven't moved workflows — at least not yet.

AI is moving from hype to pragmatism. The companies that close the gap between demo magic and actual process transformation will define the next wave of enterprise tech.

The real question is not if AI will reshape how enterprises operate. It's how fast organizations can build the governance, tooling, and AI literacy to make deployment actually work — not just in the boardroom deck, but in the systems their teams use every day.

We are at an inflection point. Time to deliver, not just demo.
