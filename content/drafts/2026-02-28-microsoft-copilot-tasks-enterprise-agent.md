## HEADLINE
Microsoft launches a background AI agent for enterprise workflows that runs on its own cloud computer, executes scheduled tasks without occupying the user's device, and reports back when complete.

## KEY FACTS
- Off-device by design: uses a cloud-based computer and browser — no performance impact on user machines, smaller attack surface than locally-running agents
- Accepts natural language instructions; supports one-time, scheduled, or recurring execution
- Requires explicit permission before "meaningful actions" (payments, sending messages)
- Source credibility: Microsoft, the vendor behind 345 million Microsoft 365 users — this is enterprise agentic AI moving from startup feature to incumbent product lifecycle

## GREY AI ANGLE
When the company that owns the enterprise desktop ships a background AI agent, the "agentic AI in the enterprise" conversation stops being a future-state discussion. The permission-before-meaningful-action guardrail is a governance design choice that mirrors the language emerging across enterprise AI deployments — this is what the governed agentic AI model looks like at scale.
