"2025 was meant to be the year agents transformed the enterprise. The hype turned out to be mostly premature."

That's not a critic talking. That's the head of Americas at one of the world's leading AI labs — admitting, out loud, that the first wave didn't land the way anyone promised.

Now comes wave two. And it looks very different.

Instead of asking every company to build custom AI agents from scratch, the new model is plug-and-play: pre-built agentic AI for finance, HR, legal, engineering, and design — deployed through enterprise-grade infrastructure with centralized admin controls, private software marketplaces, and live data connectors for tools like Gmail and DocuSign.

The finance agent does market research, competitive analysis, and financial modeling. The HR agent generates job descriptions, onboarding materials, and offer letters. Each one is a starting point your team can customize — not a one-size-fits-all bot that ignores how your organization actually works.

The stated goal: "Everybody having their own custom agent."

Here's what I think that really means for the enterprise software market. When AI labs ship department-level agents as a platform feature, the SaaS tools that currently do those jobs stop being the obvious buy. The bundling is real. The competitive threat is real.

For IT and ops leaders: your team is about to become the agent deployment and governance team. That's a new skill set, and 2026 is when it becomes a job requirement.

Is your organization ready to move from "experimenting with AI" to actually running it as operational infrastructure?
