Nvidia's moat has a price. The market just decided it's worth $500 million to find an alternative.

MatX — founded in 2023 by two former Google TPU engineers — closed a $500M Series B to build AI chips targeting 10x better LLM training and inference performance than Nvidia's current GPUs. The round was led by Jane Street, one of the most sophisticated algorithmic trading firms in the world. The co-lead is a fund founded by a former OpenAI researcher.

When Jane Street bets $500M on a semiconductor startup, it is not speculative. It is a structured bet on a structural inefficiency.

The thesis is simple: AI infrastructure built on a single supplier is a risk at scale. Nvidia's pricing reflects that leverage. Breaking the dependency is worth paying for — early.

MatX isn't alone. Etched raised $500M at a $5B valuation in January 2026 on a similar thesis. TSMC manufactures for both. The supply chain to dethrone Nvidia is being assembled in parallel by multiple well-funded teams with deep hardware pedigrees.

**For enterprise AI leaders, this changes the calculus.**

Today, AI compute economics run through Nvidia. In 2027 — when MatX chips are expected to ship — that may no longer be the only option. Companies investing now in AI infrastructure at scale should be modeling a future where chip diversity exists and the cost curve moves.

The race to own AI compute is not between Nvidia and one challenger. It is Nvidia versus a coordinated wave.
