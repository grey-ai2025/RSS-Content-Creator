## HEADLINE
OpenAI closes $110 billion in private funding at a $730 billion valuation, backed by Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank, with infrastructure commitments totaling 7GW of compute.

## KEY FACTS
- $110 billion raised in a single round — more than double the previous record round ($40B at $300B valuation, under 12 months ago)
- Amazon: $50 billion investment plus a commitment to 2GW of Trainium compute; Nvidia: $30 billion plus 3GW dedicated inference and 2GW Vera Rubin training
- ChatGPT now counts 900 million weekly active users and 50 million+ paid subscribers
- Source credibility: Major investments confirmed by Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank — three of the largest AI infrastructure players globally

## GREY AI ANGLE
The infrastructure scale here reframes AI vendor selection as a strategic bet — enterprises now need to evaluate not just model capability but whether their AI provider has the compute runway to remain competitive at scale. Organizations that haven't yet mapped their AI stack to vendor stability are behind.

## RAW MATERIAL
$730 billion pre-money valuation — against $300 billion less than a year ago. Amazon's $35 billion tranche is contingent on OpenAI achieving AGI or completing its IPO by year-end. The combined infrastructure commitments (Amazon 2GW + Nvidia 5GW) represent a bet on compute dominance, not just model R&D. Andy Jassy: "our unique collaboration with OpenAI to provide stateful runtime environments will change what's possible for customers building AI apps and agents."
