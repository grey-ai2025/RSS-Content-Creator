Plot twist: the most interesting AI model launched this week wasn't from OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google.

It was from a Finnish ring company.

Oura just launched a proprietary AI model purpose-built for women's health — covering questions spanning the full reproductive health spectrum, from early menstrual cycles through menopause. Not layered on top of a commodity API. Built from scratch, trained on data that actually reflects women's health journeys.

This is smaller models, bigger impact in action.

General-purpose AI is impressive at general-purpose things. But when a woman asks a nuanced question about perimenopause symptoms alongside her sleep and heart rate data, "let me check my training data from Reddit" is not a satisfying answer. A domain-specific model, trained on relevant data with real clinical grounding, will beat GPT-4 at this task every time.

That's the shift: competing on AI quality and specificity, not just hardware specs or follower count.

Women's health is also a historically underserved category in both consumer tech and healthcare AI. Building a proprietary model here isn't just a smart product decision — it's a potential moat in a market where trust and accuracy are the only things that matter.

For AI wearables and health tech, Oura just raised the bar. The next frontier isn't who has the biggest model. It's who has the right model for the person wearing the device.

AI literacy is the new baseline. And apparently, so is knowing when to go bespoke instead of off-the-shelf.
