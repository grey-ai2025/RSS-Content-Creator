## HEADLINE
Block cuts 40% of its workforce — more than 4,000 jobs — with its CEO explicitly citing AI capability gains, not financial pressure, as the reason.

## KEY FACTS
- 4,000+ jobs eliminated from a 10,000+ person workforce; stock rose 25%+ in after-hours trading on the announcement
- Block reported strong Q4 2025 financials ($6.3 billion revenue) — this was a strategic restructuring, not a crisis response
- CEO publicly predicted "a majority of companies" will make the same move within a year
- Source credibility: Named CEO, named company, public shareholder letter — the first explicit major-CEO statement tying AI capability directly to headcount reduction

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the clearest signal yet that AI adoption isn't just about productivity gains — it's actively reshaping what headcount organizations believe they need. For teams still in "pilot mode," Block is the case study that changes the board conversation: AI readiness is now a workforce strategy question, not just a technology one.
