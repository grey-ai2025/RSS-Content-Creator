2025 was supposed to be the year AI agents transformed the enterprise. It wasn't.

Anthropic's own head of Americas said it plainly: "It wasn't a failure of effort. It was a failure of approach."

That admission is rarer than it sounds. Most AI companies retcon their roadmaps. Anthropic named the problem — and shipped the fix.

The new Claude Cowork plug-in system deploys pre-built agents across finance, legal, HR, engineering, and design — connected directly to Gmail, DocuSign, Google Workspace, and Clay. Not standalone demos. Agents embedded in the systems enterprises already run, pulling live data, executing multi-step tasks end-to-end across Excel and PowerPoint without a human handoff.

This changes the calculus for enterprise software vendors.

**Finance plug-ins** handle market research, financial modeling, and data flows.
**HR plug-ins** draft job descriptions, onboarding materials, and offer letters.
**Legal and engineering** follow the same pattern: vertical-specific, IT-controlled, built to replace category leaders — not supplement them.

The frame that matters here is not "AI as assistant." It is "AI as SaaS replacement."

Every category-leading enterprise software vendor now has to answer the same question: what happens to your product when a Claude agent can do its core job inside the tools your customers already use — without a migration, a new contract, or a training program?

The answer arrives sometime in 2026. 🎯
