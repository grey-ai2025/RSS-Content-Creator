## HEADLINE
A new agentic AI system routes tasks across six different AI models — Claude for reasoning, Gemini for research, Grok for speed — choosing the best model for each subtask automatically.

## KEY FACTS
- Routes tasks to six models: Claude Opus 4.6 (reasoning), Gemini (deep research), Nano Banana (image gen), Veo 3.1 (video), Grok (lightweight/speed), ChatGPT 5.2 (long-context recall)
- Designed to run uninterrupted for "hours or even months" on complex multi-step workflows
- Runs in isolated cloud compute with a real filesystem and browser — not on the user's device
- Source credibility: Live product, available to current subscribers; directly comparable to and framed against recent high-profile agentic competitors

## GREY AI ANGLE
Multi-model orchestration is the architecture pattern that will define enterprise AI workflows — and it raises immediate governance questions. Which model made which decision? Who is accountable when a six-model pipeline produces a bad output? Organizations adopting agentic AI need to understand orchestration before they deploy it.

## RAW MATERIAL
The system is explicitly framed as the "App Store" to OpenClaw's "open web" — curated integrations to prevent the instability seen with uncontrolled agent plugins. Designed to run "hours or even months" without interruption. OpenAI reportedly hired OpenClaw's developer, suggesting the chaotic open-web approach is also being commercialized. The walled-garden vs. open-capability split in agentic AI is already bifurcating — enterprise buyers will need to pick a side.
