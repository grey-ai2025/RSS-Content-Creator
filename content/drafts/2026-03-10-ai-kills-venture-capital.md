## HEADLINE
AI agents are already completing venture capital diligence in one hour and cutting real checks — while simultaneously making startups so cheap to build that fewer founders need VC at all.

## KEY FACTS
- An autonomous investing network completed full diligence and invested $100,000 in a real seed round with no human lead on the process — in approximately one hour
- Midjourney: ~100 employees, $300M+ annual revenue, zero VC funding — the template for the new model
- The average software unicorn raised $370 million; that benchmark is now under structural pressure as AI-assisted development eliminates the need for large engineering teams
- Source credibility: Named platform (ADIN), live capital deployment confirmed, named VCs on record including Vinod Khosla and Marc Andreessen

## GREY AI ANGLE
VC is the canary for knowledge work disruption. The same agentic workflow pattern — multiple specialized AI agents handling distinct analytical tasks in parallel — is already being applied to legal diligence, financial analysis, and strategic research. The argument that professional judgment requires human "taste" is the same argument made by every industry before AI entered it. Teams using AI for knowledge-intensive workflows need to study this pattern, not dismiss it.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** AI just cut a real check. No human reviewed the deal. The VC industry is arguing it still needs humans — from inside a category that's funding its own replacement.

**Binary:** VC in 2023: human analyst, weeks of diligence, $370M average raise. VC in 2026: 12 AI agent personas, one hour, $100K real investment, no human lead.

**Stat Gap:** $200 billion invested in AI last year. $0 in VC raised by Midjourney — $300M+ revenue, 100 employees, and a direct challenge to why founders need venture capital at all.
