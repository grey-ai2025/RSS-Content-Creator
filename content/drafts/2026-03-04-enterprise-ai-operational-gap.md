## HEADLINE
A survey of 500 IT leaders finds three in four companies have AI in production — but two-thirds have no dedicated team maintaining it, and Gartner predicts 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027.

## KEY FACTS
- 76% of companies have at least one AI workflow fully in production; only 34% have a dedicated team to maintain it — the other 66% are running production AI without dedicated ownership
- Companies with enterprise-wide integration platforms are 5x more likely to use 5+ diverse data sources in AI workflows (59% vs. 11% for workflow-only integrations, vs. 0% for no integration platform)
- 43% of organizations find AI most successful when applied to well-defined, established processes — not new or chaotic ones
- Source credibility: MIT Technology Review Insights survey of 500 senior IT leaders, December 2025; Gartner forecast corroborating the cancellation risk

## GREY AI ANGLE
The operational gap is an AI literacy gap in disguise. Most organizations have figured out how to run an AI pilot. Almost none have built the integration infrastructure, governance ownership, and process discipline that production-grade AI agents actually require. The 5x data-source multiplier is the clearest indicator: model selection is not the bottleneck — operational foundation is.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** 76% of companies have AI in production. 66% have nobody responsible for maintaining it. That math ends badly.

**Stat Gap:** With the right integration infrastructure: 59% use 5+ data sources. Without it: 0%. The gap is not the AI — it's the plumbing.

**Binary:** AI pilot success: model selection, a few enthusiastic engineers, one working demo. AI production success: dedicated ownership, enterprise integration, governance design. Most organizations have only done one of these.

## RAW MATERIAL
Survey core finding: "The real issue is not the AI itself, but the missing operational foundation." 43% of organizations succeed by applying AI to already-automated, established workflows — not by trying to automate chaotic processes first. Only 34% have a dedicated AI workflow maintenance team; 21% rely on central IT, 25% on departmental operations, 19% spread responsibility across functions. Gartner: over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027 due to cost, inaccuracy, and governance challenges. The 5x multiplier: enterprise-wide integration platforms enable 59% of organizations to use 5+ data sources — compared to 11% with workflow-specific integrations and 0% with no integration platform.
