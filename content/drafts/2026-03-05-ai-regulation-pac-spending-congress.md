## HEADLINE
A Silicon Valley-backed super PAC has raised $125 million specifically to defeat state-level AI regulation candidates — spending $10 million to oppose a single assembly race in a district where total campaign budgets typically run under $100,000.

## KEY FACTS
- "Leading the Future" PAC: $125M raised, backed by OpenAI's president, Andreessen Horowitz, Palantir's co-founder, and Perplexity
- $10M committed against a single New York Assembly candidate whose AI bill covers only labs with $500M+ revenue, requires safety plans and incident reporting, and prohibits nothing
- The pro-regulation candidate's entire support: $450,000 — a 22:1 spending disadvantage
- Source credibility: FEC filings, named funders, named PAC — not allegations, documented capital deployment

## GREY AI ANGLE
Enterprise AI governance teams have been operating on the assumption that the regulatory environment is a slow-moving technical process. It is not. It is a politically contested arena where the outcome is being shaped right now by capital deployment at unprecedented scale. The organizations building AI governance frameworks need to understand what regulatory environment they will actually be operating in — not the one they assume is coming.

**Binary:** AI governance written in legislative chambers: slow, fragmented, underfunded. AI governance shaped by $125 million super PACs: moving fast, well-funded, and pointed at specific people.

## RAW MATERIAL
The RAISE Act — the bill under attack — covers only AI labs with $500M+ in revenue, requires safety plans and reporting of catastrophic incidents, and prohibits no specific use. Meta deployed $65 million into two separate state-level pro-tech PACs. AI companies and executives donated $83 million to federal campaigns in 2025 alone. Counter-spending from Anthropic's allied group: $450,000. The targeted candidate would be only the second member of Congress with a computer science degree — meaning the spend is explicitly aimed at removing technical literacy from the legislative body overseeing AI.
