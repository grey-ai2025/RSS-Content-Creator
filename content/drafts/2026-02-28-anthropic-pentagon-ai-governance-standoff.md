## HEADLINE
Anthropic refuses Pentagon demand to remove AI safety guardrails, risking designation as a national security supply chain threat.

## KEY FACTS
- The DoD demanded "any lawful use" access — including mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous lethal decisions with no human in the kill chain
- Penalty threatened: "supply chain risk" designation that could cost hundreds of billions in government contracts
- OpenAI and xAI reportedly agreed to the DoD's terms; OpenAI also reportedly seeking the same carve-outs Anthropic held
- Source credibility: First major public confrontation between an AI company and the US military over guardrail removal; confirmed across multiple news outlets

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the most consequential AI governance story of 2026: it establishes that AI vendor red lines are not internal compliance choices — they are geopolitical positions. Every enterprise AI buyer now needs to ask what their vendor will and won't agree to under contract pressure, and who enforces it.
