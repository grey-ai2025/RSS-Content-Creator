## HEADLINE
AI agents are breaking the per-seat pricing model that built the SaaS industry — because agents don't need seats, and AI coding tools are making custom software cheap enough to build instead of buy.

## KEY FACTS
- A single wave of AI product launches wiped nearly $1 trillion in market value from software and services stocks in early February 2026
- Klarna replaced a major CRM platform with a homegrown AI system — a high-profile early signal of the "build versus buy" shift going enterprise
- No venture-backed SaaS IPO filings are on the horizon for 2026, despite a thawing IPO market — per Crunchbase
- Source credibility: Finastra survey (1,509 executives), Crunchbase market data, on-record VC partners including Emergence Capital and F-Prime

## GREY AI ANGLE
The SaaS disruption story is an organizational readiness story in disguise. Every enterprise technology leader who hasn't audited their software stack against AI-native alternatives is now operating with outdated buy-versus-build logic. Model Context Protocol is commoditizing integrations. Per-seat pricing is becoming indefensible. The teams that understand these structural shifts will negotiate differently, build differently, and budget differently.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** SaaS charged you per seat. AI agents don't have seats. The business model is broken.

**Binary:** Per-seat pricing: designed for human workers. Outcome-based pricing: designed for AI agents. One of these is the future.

**Contrarian:** The SaaS companies most at risk aren't the ones AI can replace. They're the ones whose entire moat was the integration layer — and MCP just made that a utility.
