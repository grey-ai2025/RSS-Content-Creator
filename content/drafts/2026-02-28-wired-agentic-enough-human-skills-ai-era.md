## HEADLINE
Silicon Valley is redefining the most valuable human skill in the AI era: not coding, not data science — the ability to set goals, delegate to agents, and course-correct when outputs diverge.

## KEY FACTS
- AI coding agents can now independently write, test, and iterate on code — the cognitive premium has shifted from execution to direction
- AI-native companies are actively prioritizing hiring for workers who can direct multi-agent workflows over those who execute individual tasks
- "Agentic" is being applied to people as well as AI systems — Silicon Valley is adopting self-directed initiative as a people-selection metric
- Source credibility: Synthesis of hiring trends at AI-native companies, corroborated by LinkedIn's 2026 Skills on the Rise data showing AI engineering and prompt engineering as top priorities

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the AI literacy framing Grey AI exists to address: the question is no longer "can your team use AI tools" but "can your team direct AI systems toward the right goals." Organizational readiness now means building the judgment layer — the human who decides what the AI should work toward — not just the technical layer.

## RAW MATERIAL
Wired framing: "The worker who survives is the one who decides what the AI does, not the one who does what the AI now can." The "agentic" individual — someone who acts with self-direction and initiative — is becoming a hiring criterion at AI-native companies, not just a model capability descriptor. LinkedIn's 2026 Skills on the Rise: AI engineering, MLOps, and prompt engineering are top hiring priorities. Block's Jack Dorsey explicitly cited AI capability gains — not performance — as the reason for 40% workforce reduction. The gap between "machine that executes" and "human who sets intent" remains real regardless of how capable the machine becomes.
