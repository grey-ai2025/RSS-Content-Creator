## HEADLINE
Anthropic filed a federal lawsuit challenging its designation as a national supply-chain risk — a label historically reserved for Chinese technology companies, not US AI labs.

## KEY FACTS
- The supply-chain-risk authority being used (DFARS Subpart 239.73) has never previously been applied to a domestic US company
- Anthropic faces potential loss of hundreds of millions in annual government revenue; several defense contractors including Lockheed have begun replacing Claude
- A coalition spanning Apple, Google, Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, IBM, Salesforce, and Oracle formally urged the administration to reverse the designation
- Source credibility: Federal court filing, named defense companies, named former intelligence officials, named defense secretary on record

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the first legal test of whether AI governance policies are protected speech or contract violations. The outcome will define whether enterprise AI vendors can set ethical limits on government use — or whether accepting unrestricted deployment becomes the price of doing business. Every organization building an AI governance policy is watching this case.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** A US AI company is now classified the same way as Chinese spyware. That happened because it said no to autonomous weapons.

**Binary:** Accept unrestricted government deployment: get the contract. Draw an ethical line: get designated a national security threat. That's the choice AI vendors now face.

**Contrarian:** The most dangerous precedent in AI governance isn't a regulation. It's a procurement label that's never been used against an American company before.

## RAW MATERIAL
DFARS Subpart 239.73 has historically excluded Chinese firms like Huawei and ZTE — never a US company. Anthropic's legal argument: it was singled out when rival OpenAI secured a deal with functionally identical contractual constraints. Microsoft confirmed it will continue offering Claude to all US agencies except the DoD. Defense Secretary Hegseth: phasing out Anthropic could take up to six months. Former CIA Director Michael Hayden and multiple retired military admirals signed a letter opposing the designation. Dario Amodei confirmed "productive conversations" are continuing even as the lawsuit proceeds.
