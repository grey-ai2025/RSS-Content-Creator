## HEADLINE
A British AI infrastructure company just raised the largest Series C in European history, with OpenAI as a customer and Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and Nvidia as backers — at a $14.6 billion valuation.

## KEY FACTS
- $2 billion Series C plus a $1.4 billion GPU-backed term loan — $3.4 billion in new capital in one cycle
- Stargate Norway joint venture targeting 100,000 Nvidia GPUs by end of 2026; Microsoft deal covers approximately 200,000 Nvidia GPUs across European and US data centers
- Board additions: former Meta COO, former UK Deputy PM, former Yahoo president — a deliberate geopolitical credibility signal
- Source credibility: Named investors (Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Nvidia, Citadel), named anchor customer (OpenAI), CEO on record re IPO timeline

## GREY AI ANGLE
The question enterprise technology leaders have been treating as theoretical — where does your AI compute actually run, under whose jurisdiction — is being answered by $14.6 billion in institutional capital. European sovereign AI infrastructure is no longer a policy aspiration; it is a funded commercial alternative. Organizations evaluating AI deployment need a jurisdictional layer in their vendor strategy.

**Binary:** Rent compute from a US hyperscaler and accept their jurisdiction. Or build sovereign infrastructure. Institutional capital just made the second option real.
