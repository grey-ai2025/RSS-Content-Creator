## HEADLINE
Nvidia's CEO confirmed the company is stepping back from private AI lab investments — after its OpenAI stake was described as "a wash" by analysts, and its Anthropic stake became an equity position in a company the US government designated a national security risk.

## KEY FACTS
- Nvidia pledged $100 billion into OpenAI; the position it actually took in the $110B round was $30 billion — a $70 billion gap between announcement and execution
- Anthropic CEO publicly compared Nvidia's China chip sales to "selling nuclear weapons to North Korea" at Davos — weeks before the Trump administration blacklisted Anthropic as a supply chain risk
- An MIT Sloan professor characterized the Nvidia-OpenAI deal as "kind of a wash" — Nvidia investing in its own chip customer, recirculating its own revenue
- Source credibility: Named CEO statement at Morgan Stanley conference, MIT Sloan analysis, public Davos remarks, documented Trump administration blacklist

## GREY AI ANGLE
The AI infrastructure stack is not a neutral utility — it is a geopolitically exposed investment ecosystem where the dominant chip supplier is now disentangling from the labs it helped build, for reasons it is not fully disclosing. Enterprise leaders selecting AI infrastructure vendors need to understand this: the supply chain they depend on is under political and competitive pressure that a product roadmap will not reveal.

## RAW MATERIAL
MIT Sloan professor on the OpenAI deal: "kind of a wash" — OpenAI would use the capital to buy Nvidia chips, Nvidia would gain a stake in its own customer. Nvidia's stated IPO-window rationale for exiting is inconsistent with standard late-stage private investing behavior — the IPO premium typically makes stakes more attractive to hold, not less. Wall Street is flagging growing concern about "circular AI deals" and whether large announced commitments reflect genuine demand or investment bubble mechanics. The Trump administration's Anthropic blacklist put Nvidia in the position of holding equity in a company simultaneously designated as a federal supply chain risk and a commercial beneficiary of that same political conflict (Claude hit #2 in the App Store).
