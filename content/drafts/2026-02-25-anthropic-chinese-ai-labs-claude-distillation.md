24,000 fake accounts. One very annoyed AI company. A geopolitical incident hiding in your API logs.

Anthropic has accused three Chinese AI labs — DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — of running a large-scale "model distillation" operation against Claude. The alleged scheme: create ~24,000 fake accounts, spam Claude with queries, harvest the outputs, and use them as training data to bootstrap competing models.

It's less Mission Impossible and more "extremely aggressive web scraping with extra steps."

Model distillation itself isn't new — it's a known technique where a smaller model learns from a larger one's outputs. What's new is doing it adversarially, at scale, against a frontier lab's production system. It's the AI equivalent of reverse-engineering a product by buying a lot of it and taking it apart in your lab.

This story lands while Congress is actively debating AI chip export controls aimed at slowing China's AI development. Anthropic's disclosure adds a pointed data point to that conversation: controlling hardware exports might matter less if the model capabilities can be extracted through the front door.

For enterprise teams using AI APIs: your model's outputs are increasingly valuable IP. This story is a reminder that the competitive landscape around AI isn't just about who builds the best model — it's about who can protect it.
