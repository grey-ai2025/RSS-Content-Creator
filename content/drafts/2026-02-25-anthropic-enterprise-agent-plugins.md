Your SaaS stack just received a politely worded eviction notice.

Anthropic launched domain-specific Claude plugins this week targeting finance, engineering, and design — three verticals where purpose-built software tools have lived comfortably for years. TechCrunch called it "a significant threat to SaaS products currently performing those functions," which is journalist-speak for "things are about to get awkward at a lot of board meetings."

The strategic move here is vertical integration. Instead of Claude being a general assistant you bolt onto your existing tools, Anthropic is saying: what if Claude just... did the whole workflow? Specialized. Agentic. Actually useful for domain-specific tasks, not just asking it to summarize your emails.

For product teams and enterprise software vendors, this is the "AI eats software" thesis playing out in real time. AI agents that can manage finance workflows or run engineering tasks don't replace your team — they replace the subscriptions your team uses to do those things.

For professionals in these fields: this is a productivity unlock worth evaluating seriously. For SaaS founders in those three verticals: maybe worth a second look at your moat.

The age of the general-purpose AI assistant was fun. The age of the agent that does your actual job is here.
