## HEADLINE
Nvidia committed $26 billion over five years to open-weight frontier model development and is simultaneously launching NemoClaw — an enterprise AI agent platform with native security controls — to compete directly at the orchestration layer of agentic AI.

## KEY FACTS
- $26 billion committed to open-weight model development (confirmed in SEC filings); a 550B-parameter model already pretrained
- Nemotron 3 Super (128B parameters) scores 37 on the AI Index vs. GPT-OSS's 33 — already outperforming on benchmarks
- NemoClaw includes built-in security and privacy controls; being pitched to Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike ahead of developer conference
- Source credibility: SEC filings, named VP Bryan Catanzaro on record, named corporate partners

## GREY AI ANGLE
Nvidia is no longer a picks-and-shovels play. The $26B model bet and NemoClaw reframe the enterprise procurement conversation from "which GPU?" to "which agent platform?" — and directly challenge Chinese open models (DeepSeek, Alibaba Qwen) attracting global developers. For enterprise AI leaders, the agent orchestration layer is now a multi-vendor competitive market, not a default choice.
