One blog post. One 13% stock crash. Billions in market cap, gone before lunch.

Anthropic published a piece explaining how Claude Code can modernize legacy COBOL codebases — mapping dependencies, documenting workflows, flagging migration risks — in a fraction of the time human analysts would need. The market heard "IBM's mainframe business just became a lot less necessary" and hit sell.

IBM shares closed down ~13.2% to $223.35. Worst single-day drop in 25 years.

Here's the delicious irony: IBM launched its own COBOL-to-Java AI conversion tool — watsonx Code Assistant for Z — back in 2023. They literally had the same pitch. IBM's CEO even credited AI conversion tools with delivering the company's highest mainframe revenue in 20 years.

But when Anthropic says it? Apparently the market believes it more.

The real story here isn't IBM vs. Anthropic. It's that AI code generation is now moving fast enough to put multi-billion-dollar legacy businesses on notice in a single news cycle. COBOL runs the world's banks, airlines, and government systems. That's a huge modernization market — and if AI can compress years of migration work into weeks, the economics of outsourced mainframe services change fundamentally.

We are at an inflection point. The question isn't whether AI disrupts legacy enterprise IT. It's which vendors survive the transition — and which ones get blog-posted into oblivion.

This changes the developer's role forever. And apparently, the CFO's weekend plans too.
