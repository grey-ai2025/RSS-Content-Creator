## HEADLINE
One of the world's largest professional services firms has made AI proficiency a formal requirement for employee promotion — removing it from the optional skills category entirely.

## KEY FACTS
- AI skills are now a stated promotion requirement across the firm's global workforce — not a differentiator, a gate
- Recommendation attached: fix broken processes before deploying AI on top of them — AI amplifies dysfunction as readily as it amplifies efficiency
- Firm is continuing entry-level hiring even as AI automates more tasks — counter-narrative to pure displacement framing
- Source credibility: Named CEO, on-record interview, corroborated by LinkedIn data showing 70% YoY increase in US job postings requiring AI literacy

## GREY AI ANGLE
This is the formal institutionalization of AI literacy as a career requirement. The implication for every organization is structural: if the largest professional services firms are gatekeeping promotion on AI competency, their clients — across every industry — will face the same expectation from boards and investors. The window to build AI literacy proactively, before it becomes a performance criterion, is measurably closing.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** AI skills just moved from your LinkedIn "nice to have" section to the list of things that block your next promotion.

**Binary:** Before: AI literacy as a differentiator — the person who knows more gets ahead. After: AI literacy as a gate — the person who doesn't know enough doesn't advance.

**Contrarian:** Most companies are still running optional AI training programs. One of the world's largest firms just made AI skills a promotion requirement. Those are two different organizational philosophies — and one of them will become the norm.
