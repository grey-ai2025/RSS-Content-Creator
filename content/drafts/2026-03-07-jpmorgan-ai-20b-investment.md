## HEADLINE
The world's largest bank is spending nearly $20 billion on technology in 2026 — with $1.2 billion specifically allocated to AI — and its CFO is directly attributing revenue impact to machine-learning deployments across trading, lending, fraud, and operations.

## KEY FACTS
- Technology budget approaching $19.8 billion in 2026; approximately $1.2 billion earmarked for AI-related initiatives
- CFO on record: machine-learning analytics are now contributing directly to revenue — not just cost savings
- AI deployed across four high-stakes production functions: trading (price pattern analysis), lending (credit risk), fraud detection (near real-time), operations (contract review, research summarization)
- Source credibility: Named CFO, named bank, public-facing investment figures; Gartner and MIT Technology Review survey data corroborating the enterprise execution gap

## GREY AI ANGLE
JPMorgan's investment pattern is the enterprise AI execution template: start with clearly defined business problems, deploy where financial returns are measurable, build dedicated infrastructure for maintenance. The stat that cuts against the optimism — only 34% of organizations have a dedicated team maintaining their AI workflows — reveals why most companies won't replicate what JPMorgan is doing. The gap between AI ambition and AI execution is structural, not motivational.
