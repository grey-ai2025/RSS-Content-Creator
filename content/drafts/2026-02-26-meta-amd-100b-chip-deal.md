Meta didn't just buy chips. It bought a stake in its own supply chain.

The company announced a multiyear deal to purchase up to $100 billion in AMD chips — and structured it so AMD issued Meta a warrant for approximately 160 million shares (roughly 10% of the company) at $0.01 each, vesting on performance milestones.

That is not procurement. That is vertical integration through equity.

The deal covers AMD's MI540 GPU series and latest-generation CPUs, covering roughly 6 gigawatts of data center power demand. Mark Zuckerberg tied it explicitly to Meta's stated goal: building "personal superintelligence" — AI designed to deeply understand and empower individual users at scale.

Three things this deal makes clear:

**Hyperscalers are diversifying before they need to.** Meta also holds Nvidia contracts. This is deliberate redundancy — not a pivot away from Nvidia, but a hedge against Nvidia's pricing leverage.

**CPUs are becoming a first-class AI compute layer.** AMD CEO Lisa Su said the CPU market is "absolutely on fire" for agentic AI workloads. Inference at scale requires different hardware than training — and the architecture is evolving fast.

**"Personal superintelligence" is no longer a research concept.** It is a stated corporate objective attached to a $100B procurement decision and a $135B 2026 capex plan.

The AI infrastructure buildout is not slowing. The companies that control compute control the future of AI-first strategy — and Meta is making sure it controls its own.
