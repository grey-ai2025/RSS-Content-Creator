## HEADLINE
Google embedded Gemini into Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drive with full-draft generation, cross-document Q&A, and natural-language spreadsheet creation — moving AI from a sidebar tool to the primary interface.

## KEY FACTS
- "Help me create" in Docs pulls context from Gmail, Chat, Drive, and the web to generate a complete first draft from a single prompt
- Sheets generates fully formatted spreadsheets — including populated data tables — from one natural-language description
- "Ask Gemini in Drive" answers complex questions across emails, calendar entries, and uploaded files simultaneously
- Source credibility: Google official rollout; Gemini scored 70.48% on SpreadsheetBench — highest of any AI on real-world spreadsheet tasks

## GREY AI ANGLE
Workspace is where most knowledge workers spend 80% of their day. When the AI moves from a "side-panel novelty" to the primary draft, formula, and search interface, AI literacy is no longer optional for office professionals — it is the new baseline for doing the job. Teams that haven't built Gemini familiarity into onboarding workflows are already behind the adoption curve.

## HOOK OPTIONS

**Provocative:** Gemini just became the default first author in Google Docs. The question isn't whether your team will use it — it's whether they know how.

**Binary:** Old Workspace: open a blank doc, start typing. New Workspace: describe what you need, Gemini drafts it from your emails, files, and calendar. Two different jobs.

**Stat Gap:** Gemini: 70.48% on real-world spreadsheet benchmarks. Nearest competitor: lower. The productivity gap is already measurable — most teams haven't noticed yet.

## RAW MATERIAL
"Help me create" refines specific sections on request without regenerating the full document — targeted editing, not just regeneration. "Match writing style" unifies tone across multi-author documents. "Fill with Gemini" in Sheets populates tables with categorized data or real-time web information. WIRED hands-on: features were fast and accurate for data retrieval, but AI prose had "corporate-speak" tendencies. Rolling out to Google AI Ultra and Pro subscribers in English worldwide; Drive features US-only initially. Google's own framing: moving Gemini from "side-panel novelty" to "collaborative partner."
