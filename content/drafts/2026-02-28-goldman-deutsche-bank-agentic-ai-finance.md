## HEADLINE
Goldman Sachs and Deutsche Bank are running live agentic AI pilots in trade surveillance — replacing static compliance rules with agents that reason across multi-signal data streams.

## KEY FACTS
- Deutsche Bank's agents analyze order data, execution data, trade timing, market conditions, and individual trader history simultaneously — in near real time
- Goldman Sachs is exploring agentic AI that flags misconduct patterns with no predefined rules — pattern recognition without a rulebook
- Human compliance officers retain final review authority; AI functions as an enhanced detection layer only
- Source credibility: Both are among the world's most compliance-sensitive institutions, running live pilots in their most scrutinized operations

## GREY AI ANGLE
Finance is deploying agentic AI at its most sensitive and regulated layer — and the governance model they're using (AI expands detection surface; humans compress to judgment calls only) is a template every enterprise sector will follow. Understanding this architecture is foundational for any team implementing AI in regulated or high-stakes workflows.
