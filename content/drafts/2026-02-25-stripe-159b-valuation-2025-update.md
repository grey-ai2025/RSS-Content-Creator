Stripe processed $1.9 trillion in 2025. For context, that's roughly 1.6% of global GDP running through two brothers from Tipperary.

The company dropped its 2025 annual letter this week alongside a tender offer valuing it at $159 billion — providing liquidity to employees via heavyweights like a16z, Coatue, and Thrive Capital. The growth numbers are legitimately wild: 34% year-over-year volume increase, new business cohorts reaching $10M ARR within 3 months at double the rate of 2024, and 57%+ of new businesses joining from outside the US.

But the most interesting line in the letter isn't the valuation. It's this: Stripe co-developed an Agentic Commerce Protocol with OpenAI.

AI agents that can transact on your behalf. That's not a feature — that's a new layer of the internet's economic plumbing. When your AI assistant can pay for things autonomously, the infrastructure underneath needs to be rebuilt from scratch. Stripe is already planting its flag.

Stablecoin volume also doubled to ~$400B, with 60% being B2B. The "crypto is for speculation" narrative is aging poorly.

Stripe's numbers aren't just impressive — they're a proxy for how fast the internet economy is growing. And it's accelerating.
