## HEADLINE
A cross-ideological coalition of 90+ labor, religious, and civic leaders — deliberately excluding all tech company representatives — has published a five-principle AI governance declaration with 80%+ public support on every clause.

## KEY FACTS
- The least popular of the five principles still received 94% approval from attendees; general voter polling shows the worst-performing principle at 69% support — well above any legislative threshold
- Signatories include AFL-CIO, Screen Writers Guild, SAG-AFTRA, Congress of Christian Leaders, and individual signatories spanning Ralph Nader to Steve Bannon — the first such cross-ideological alignment on AI governance
- The 5 principles target: human agency, power concentration, child/family protection, AI legal personhood, and autonomous lethal weapons — all enterprise-relevant compliance risk areas
- Source credibility: Future of Life Institute, Chatham House Rules conference, February 2026 Tavern Research poll

## GREY AI ANGLE
When labor unions, conservative think tanks, and religious organizations sign the same governance document, that is not a fringe political signal — it is the leading indicator of bipartisan legislation. Organizations need to audit their AI deployments against these five principles now, because this coalition has the cross-aisle structure to make them compliance requirements within 2-3 years.
