# SEO Keywords — 2026-03-07

## Trending Topics

Keywords below are ordered by estimated relevance (most relevant first).

1. **OpenClaw / agentic AI tools** — OpenClaw (the viral open-source AI agent formerly called Clawdbot/MoltBot) is dominating developer conversation this week. A critical security vulnerability was patched, AWS launched it on Lightsail, and Google made Workspace "agent-ready" for it. The platform-level infrastructure story is accelerating fast.

2. **AI agents — enterprise adoption** — Agentic AI is the single biggest enterprise technology story of early 2026. Firms like Workday, Dialpad, and Luma are shipping production agent platforms. Analysts are framing 2026 as the transition from GenAI experimentation to agentic AI execution.

3. **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** — MCP is emerging as the connective tissue of the agentic AI stack. Cross-agent communication, MCP adoption, and multi-agent orchestration are being called out explicitly as 2026's key architectural shift by enterprise analysts.

4. **AI-driven layoffs and workforce displacement** — Block laid off 4,000 of 10,000 employees citing AI efficiency gains. Burger King testing AI headsets to monitor workers. The AI-labor substitution story is breaking into mainstream business coverage with hard numbers.

5. **AI security and prompt injection** — The OpenClaw ClawJacked vulnerability and Palo Alto's Unit 42 research on indirect prompt injection (IDPI) attacks are driving serious enterprise security conversation. AI agent attack surfaces are a fast-growing professional concern.

6. **Apple M5 chip / silicon performance** — Apple M5 Max broke Geekbench records, topping all consumer PC processors. MacBook Neo pre-orders are selling out. Apple silicon's performance trajectory is a major tech story with enterprise and developer implications.

7. **AI and hiring — LinkedIn Skills on the Rise 2026** — LinkedIn's own data shows AI has added 1.3 million jobs globally. AI Engineer is one of the fastest-growing roles. The "AI creates jobs" counter-narrative is getting traction against the displacement story.

8. **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** — As AI handles an estimated 25% of global queries in 2026, enterprise SEO is evolving into GEO — optimizing content to be cited by AI systems rather than ranked in blue links. Growing fast among B2B marketers.

9. **AI governance and enterprise AI strategy** — Deloitte's State of AI 2026 report and MIT Sloan analysis both flag that enterprise AI execution is lagging adoption. Governance frameworks, data quality, and formal AI strategy are becoming board-level concerns.

10. **Microsoft Copilot integration expansion** — Copilot is embedding a browser side panel directly in Windows, moving from assistant to ambient computing layer. The "Copilot everywhere" story is intensifying across the Microsoft ecosystem.

11. **Luma AI / multimodal creative agents** — Luma launched Luma Agents this week — end-to-end creative AI agents across text, image, video, and audio powered by its Unified Intelligence (Uni-1) model. A signal that creative work is the next frontier of agentic AI.

12. **AI in the military and AI ethics** — The Anthropic/Pentagon standoff over Claude AI safeguards for military use is generating significant professional discussion about where AI companies draw ethical lines.

## High-Impact Phrases

Phrases that resonate in LinkedIn professional copy. Use these naturally in post body text.

1. "from pilot to production" — The dominant enterprise AI framing of 2026; signals maturity and real business stakes vs. experimentation.

2. "the agentic era" — The phrase analysts and vendors are using to describe the post-GenAI moment. High traction in enterprise content.

3. "AI-first strategy" — Evergreen professional framing that continues to drive strong engagement; positions the writer as forward-thinking.

4. "the future of work" — High-engagement, broad-audience phrase; effective for bridging AI capability stories to workforce implications.

5. "system of action, not system of record" — Workday's framing for the shift from passive enterprise software to AI-powered execution. Concrete and thought-provoking.

6. "this changes everything" — High-engagement opener pattern on LinkedIn; use sparingly and only with a genuinely surprising data point to back it.

7. "what the numbers actually say" — Pattern that signals data-backed analysis; performs well with professional audiences skeptical of hype.

8. "the real question is" — Engagement driver; pivots from surface-level news to structural implications, which is where LinkedIn thought leadership lands.

9. "built for the agent-ready world" — Emerging phrase in enterprise AI vendor positioning; Google's framing for its Workspace CLI is seeping into broader B2B language.

10. "execution is the new experimentation" — Captures the 2026 enterprise AI shift precisely; works as a standalone claim or a section header.
