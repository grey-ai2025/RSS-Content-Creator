# SEO Keywords — 2026-03-03

## Trending Topics

Keywords below are ordered by estimated relevance (most relevant first).

1. **Agentic AI / AI agents** — The dominant AI conversation right now: MIT study flags lack of oversight, Perplexity launches "Computer" (an agent that orchestrates other agents), Cursor ships a major coding-agent update, and SaaStr publishes real deployment learnings at scale. Multi-agent systems are moving from experiment to infrastructure.

2. **Anthropic vs. US government / AI governance** — Axios reports DoD designated Anthropic a supply-chain risk, putting $60B in funding at risk. CEO Dario Amodei publicly challenged the designation. Signals a new era of AI-government tension that every enterprise AI buyer is watching.

3. **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** — Identified as a breakout enterprise trend for 2026, MCP is driving cross-agent communication and formalizing how organizations integrate AI into business workflows. Referenced across IBM, Microsoft, and EW Solutions enterprise AI analysis.

4. **AI upskilling and literacy** — LinkedIn's Skills on the Rise 2026 report shows a 70% year-over-year increase in roles requiring AI literacy. Prompt engineering, large language models, and AI business strategy are the fastest-growing skills employers are hiring for.

5. **Agentic search / Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** — AI agents are now transacting and researching on behalf of users, fundamentally changing how brands need to be discoverable. GEO (also called Answer Engine Optimization) is replacing traditional keyword SEO as the dominant strategy discussion in enterprise marketing.

6. **AI coding tools / developer AI** — Cursor's major agent update and intensifying competition in the AI coding space (GitHub Copilot, Windsurf, etc.) is a sustained high-engagement topic among developer and engineering audiences on LinkedIn.

7. **AI jobs and workforce transformation** — LinkedIn data cited by WEF shows AI has already created 1.3 million new jobs including AI Engineers, Forward-Deployed Engineers, and Data Annotators. AI-driven job creation is now a concrete counter-narrative to displacement fears.

8. **MWC 2026 / AI hardware and wearables** — Mobile World Congress 2026 produced a dense cluster of tech coverage: Qualcomm's Snapdragon Wear Elite chip, Wi-Fi 8, 6G roadmap, Lenovo AI laptops, and Nothing Phone (4a). On-device AI and contextual wearables are the hardware theme.

9. **Cloud 3.0 and AI infrastructure** — Capgemini and IBM identify a shift to hybrid, private, multi-cloud, and sovereign models designed specifically to support agentic AI workloads. Enterprise infrastructure spending is realigning around AI compute needs.

10. **AI data readiness / data fabric** — Analysts flag that most enterprises still lack AI-ready data — governed, contextualized, and aligned to use cases. "Data fabric" and "AI-ready data" are the vocabulary entering procurement and CTO conversations.

11. **AI safety and agent oversight** — MIT's study on agentic AI found significant lack of disclosure on risks, third-party testing, and controllability. Wired covered IronCurtain, an open-source secure AI agent. Safety and control are shaping enterprise buying criteria.

12. **Prompt engineering and LLM skills** — Named explicitly in LinkedIn's Skills on the Rise 2026 as top hiring targets. Alongside MLOps, Azure Databricks, PyTorch, and FastAPI, these form the technical vocabulary dominating AI talent discussion.

## High-Impact Phrases

Phrases that resonate in LinkedIn professional copy. Use these naturally in post body text.

1. "the year of truth for AI" — Signals a shift from hype to accountability; high resonance with executives evaluating AI ROI in 2026.

2. "from experimentation to infrastructure" — Captures the maturation narrative of AI agents moving into production; lands well with engineering and operations audiences.

3. "AI-ready data" — Concrete, enterprise-friendly phrase pointing to a real bottleneck; works as a hook for data governance and strategy content.

4. "expressing intent, not writing code" — Describes the paradigm shift in software development driven by AI; strong engagement with developer and CTO audiences.

5. "digital coworkers" — Accessible framing for AI agents working alongside humans; avoids both overclaiming and underclaiming.

6. "the future of work" — Evergreen high-engagement framing for AI and automation content; broad audience reach on LinkedIn.

7. "AI literacy is now a baseline skill" — Assertive, data-backed claim supported by LinkedIn's 2026 Skills on the Rise report.

8. "multi-agent systems" — Specific technical phrase capturing the next frontier beyond single AI agents; growing fast in both developer and enterprise conversations.

9. "AI-first strategy" — Clean strategic framing for business content; positions AI as a structural choice, not an add-on.

10. "this changes everything" — High-engagement opener pattern on LinkedIn; use sparingly and only when the story genuinely warrants the weight.
